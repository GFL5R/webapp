# How to Make a GFL5R Technique

A step-by-step guide for converting L5R techniques to GFL5R, preserving the soul while adapting to a firearms-first world.

---

## Overview

Converting a technique from L5R to GFL5R is a five-step process:

1. **Identify** good candidates for conversion
2. **Summarize** what the technique does in plain language
3. **Convert** the summary to the firearms-first world of GFL5R
4. **Un-summarize** using GFL5R's approaches, skills, and mechanics
5. **Verify** the soul of the technique remains intact

---

## Step 1: Identify Good Candidates

### Green Flags (Convert These)

**Clear mechanical purpose.** Techniques that do one thing well convert cleanly. A technique that says "spend opportunities to remove the Prone condition" translates directly.

**Approach-agnostic effects.** Techniques that work with any approach (or specify conditions like "when making a check") adapt easily. You just swap the L5R ring for the appropriate GFL5R approach.

**Weapon-agnostic or easily-adapted weapons.** "When making an Attack action with a readied weapon" becomes "When making an Attack action with a readied firearm" or stays as-is for melee techniques.

**Condition manipulation.** L5R conditions (Bleeding, Dazed, Disoriented, Prone) map directly to GFL5R conditions. These are the easiest conversions.

**Opportunity spend patterns.** Techniques that spend (op), (op)(op), or (op)+ translate with minimal changes.

### Yellow Flags (Proceed with Caution)

**Ring-specific mechanics.** L5R techniques tied to specific rings (Air, Earth, Fire, Water, Void) require careful mapping to GFL5R approaches. See the Ring-to-Approach mapping below.

**Honor/Glory costs.** GFL5R uses Humanity and Fame instead. Consider whether the cost makes sense in the new context, or if it should become a Fortune Point cost.

**Supernatural elements.** Invocations, kiho, and other magical techniques may need to become Remolding techniques, or be reimagined as advanced technology.

**School techniques.** These are often deeply tied to L5R's school system. Consider whether they fit GFL5R's discipline system or should be standalone techniques.

### Red Flags (Probably Don't Convert)

**Deeply setting-specific.** Techniques tied to L5R's clans, spirits, or cosmology may not translate. Ask: does this make sense in a sci-fi post-apocalyptic world?

**Mechanics that don't exist in GFL5R.** Techniques involving the Void Ring's unique mechanics, shugenja spellcasting, or other L5R-specific systems need significant reimagining.

**Already covered.** Check the existing technique list. If GFL5R already has a technique that does the same thing, you don't need to convert.

## Step 2: Summarize What It Does

Strip away the L5R-specific language and describe the technique in one or two sentences.

### Example: L5R's "Crimson Leaves Strike"

**Original:** "As an Attack action using a readied weapon, make a TN 4 Martial Arts (Earth) check against one character within range to deal physical damage equal to your Earth Ring plus bonus successes and force them to resist with a TN 4 Fitness check (Air 2, Water 5) or lose control of a chosen readied weapon, which travels 3 range bands in a direction of your choice, or take control of their weapon if you spend an Earth opportunity."

**Summary:** Attack a target, deal damage, and either knock their weapon away from them or steal it outright.

### Example: L5R's "Owl's Swoop Style"

**Original:** "While performing a Martial Arts (Melee) or Unarmed check from 1 or more range bands above your target horizontally, spend Air (op) to cause 1 target per opportunity spent to suffer the Disoriented condition."

**Summary:** When attacking from above, spend opportunities to disorient your targets.

---

## Step 3: Convert to the Firearms-First World

Now adapt the summary to GFL5R's setting and combat paradigm. This is where you decide whether the technique stays melee-focused or becomes a ranged technique.

### The Conversion Question

Ask: **Does this technique make more sense with guns, or should it remain a melee technique?**

**Convert to firearms when:**
- The technique involves range, positioning, or distance
- The "strike" could just as easily be a well-placed shot
- The technique's flavor works better with modern combat
- L5R's "ranged weapon" techniques (bows, etc.) become firearms techniques

**Keep as melee when:**
- The technique is about closing distance (gap closers)
- The technique requires physical contact (grappling, throws)
- The technique is "dripping with bladed style" (flourishes, parries)
- The technique's soul would be lost if converted to ranged

### Example Conversions

**Crimson Leaves Strike → Disarming Shot**
- *Summary:* Attack a target, deal damage, and either knock their weapon away or steal it.
- *Conversion:* A ranged version makes more sense. You're shooting to disarm, not grappling for the weapon. The "steal outright" option becomes a separate technique (Wrest Control) for close quarters.

**Owl's Swoop Style → Airborne Assault**
- *Summary:* When attacking from above, spend opportunities to disorient targets.
- *Conversion:* This works for both melee and ranged. Keep it approach-agnostic so it works whether you're dropping down with a knife or shooting from a higher position.

**Flowing Water Strike → Closing Strike**
- *Summary:* Move into melee range and deal damage, with bonus effects.
- *Conversion:* This is a gap-closer. Keep it as a melee technique. The "flowing water" imagery becomes a swift, fluid advance.

**Silencing Stroke → Hand of God**
- *Summary:* Make a precise attack that deals extra damage and goes unnoticed.
- *Conversion:* This becomes a sniper technique. The "silenced" aspect becomes about the shot being so clean and distant that no one realizes what happened.

## Step 4: Un-Summarize with GFL5R Mechanics

Now expand your converted summary into full GFL5R mechanical language.

### Ring-to-Approach Mapping

| L5R Ring | GFL5R Approach | When to Use |
|----------|----------------|-------------|
| Air | Precision | Careful, controlled actions; deception; stealth |
| Air | Swiftness | Quick reactions; reading situations; adaptability |
| Earth | Resilience | Endurance; defense; holding ground |
| Fire | Power | Aggressive actions; force; overwhelming |
| Water | Swiftness | Flowing movement; adaptation; quick thinking |
| Water | Precision | Careful observation; assessment |
| Void | Fortune | Trust in luck; accepting uncertainty; sacrifice |

**Note:** Air and Water both map to multiple approaches. Choose based on the technique's flavor:
- Air as Precision: The technique is about careful control or deception
- Air as Swiftness: The technique is about quick reactions or reading the room
- Water as Swiftness: The technique is about flowing movement or adaptation
- Water as Precision: The technique is about careful observation

### Skill Mapping

| L5R Skill | GFL5R Skill | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Martial Arts [Melee] | Blades or Hand-to-Hand | Depends on weapon type |
| Martial Arts [Ranged] | Firearms | Direct conversion |
| Martial Arts [Unarmed] | Hand-to-Hand | Direct conversion |
| Fitness | Conditioning | Physical capability |
| Command | Command | Direct conversion |
| Courtesy | Culture | Social grace |
| Sentiment | Insight | Reading people |
| Performance | Performance | Direct conversion |
| Games | Varies by game type | Chess uses Tactics, card games use Culture, shooters use Firearms, etc. Games leverage the same skills as the real-world activity |
| Commerce | Negotiation | Direct conversion |
| Skulduggery | Subterfuge, Stealth, or Deception | Depends on the act: Subterfuge for sleight-of-hand and thievery, Stealth for sneaking and hiding, Deception for lying and misdirection |
| Survival | Survival | Direct conversion |
| Medicine | Medicine | Direct conversion |
| Smithing | Crafting or Mechanics | Depends on context |
| Theology | Science or Computers | Depends on context (supernatural → tech) |

### TN Guidelines

| Difficulty | TN | When to Use |
|------------|----|----|
| Easy | 1 | Almost automatic; only fails on a bad roll |
| Routine | 2 | Standard difficulty; most trained characters succeed |
| Challenging | 3 | Requires skill; untrained characters struggle |
| Hard | 4 | Demanding; requires expertise or good dice |
| Extreme | 5 | Very difficult; only experts reliably succeed |

### Resistance Check TNs

When a technique forces a target to resist, include the alternate TNs for different approaches:

**Format:** `TN X [Skill] check (Approach1 Y, Approach2 Z)`

**Example:** `TN 4 Conditioning check (Precision 2, Swiftness 5)`

The first number is the base TN. The parenthetical shows alternate TNs when using those approaches.

### Opportunity Cost Patterns

| Cost | When to Use |
|------|-------------|
| (op) | Minor effect; one target; small bonus |
| (op)(op) | Moderate effect; multiple targets; significant bonus |
| (op)+ | Scalable effect; can spend multiple times |
| (op)(op)+ | Significant effect that can be increased |

### Rank Mapping

GFL5R compresses techniques to 1-3 ranks (instead of L5R's 1-5) because multiclassing is expected. Use this mapping:

| L5R Rank | GFL5R Rank | Notes |
|----------|------------|-------|
| 1 | 1 | Direct mapping |
| 2 | 1 or 2 | Depends on strength; weaker techniques stay at 1, stronger move to 2 |
| 3 | 2 | Direct mapping |
| 4 | 2 or 3 | Depends on strength; weaker techniques stay at 2, stronger move to 3 |
| 5 | 3 | Direct mapping |

**Decision points:** For L5R ranks 2 and 4, consider whether the technique could be scaled up or down. A rank 2 technique that's notably powerful should become rank 2 in GFL5R; one that's more basic stays at rank 1.

### Technique Structure

Every technique needs:

1. **Name** - Evocative, memorable, fitting the setting
2. **Rank** - 1 (basic), 2 (intermediate), or 3 (advanced)
3. **Type** - Combat, Command, Conditioning, Electronic Warfare, Remolding, Science, Social, Street, or Vehicle
4. **Approach** - The approach that governs the technique (or null if approach-agnostic)
5. **Skill** - The skill required (or null if skill-agnostic)
6. **Flavor** - 1-3 sentences of in-world description
7. **Description** - Full mechanical text with:
   - **Activation:** When and how to use the technique
   - **Effects:** What happens on success
   - **New Opportunities:** Additional (op) spends available

### Writing the Description

Use this template:

```
<h3>Activation:</h3>
<p>[When you can use this technique and what check to make]</p>
<h3>Effects:</h3>
<p>[What happens on success]</p>
<h3>New Opportunities:</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>[Approach] (op)[cost]:</strong> [Effect]</li>
</ul>
```

For techniques that add opportunity spends to existing checks:

```
<h3>Activation:</h3>
<p>When making a [type] check [condition], you may spend (op) as follows:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>[Approach] (op)[cost]:</strong> [Effect]</li>
</ul>
```

## Step 5: Verify the Soul

The "soul" of a technique is its core identity: what makes it memorable and distinct. After conversion, ask:

### The Soul Checklist

1. **Does it still do the same thing?** If the original technique was about disarming opponents, the converted version should still disarm opponents (even if the method changed from a sword strike to a well-placed shot).

2. **Does it still feel like the same technique?** A player familiar with the L5R version should recognize the GFL5R version as the same technique, adapted to a new setting.

3. **Did you lose anything essential?** If the original technique had a unique twist (like stealing the weapon instead of just disarming), make sure that twist survives in some form.

4. **Did you gain anything that doesn't fit?** If you added effects that weren't in the original, make sure they fit the technique's identity.

5. **Does it fit GFL5R's combat paradigm?** The technique should feel like it belongs in a firearms-first world, even if it's a melee technique.

### Soul Preservation Examples

**Crimson Leaves Strike → Disarming Shot**
- *Soul:* Attack + disarm + optional weapon theft
- *Preserved:* Attack deals damage, forces resistance check, weapon goes flying
- *Lost:* Direct weapon theft (moved to Wrest Control for close quarters)
- *Verdict:* Soul preserved. The core identity (attack that removes the opponent's weapon) remains.

**Owl's Swoop Style → Airborne Assault**
- *Soul:* Attack from above to disorient targets
- *Preserved:* Height advantage + opportunity spend to disorient
- *Adapted:* Works with any approach, not just Air
- *Verdict:* Soul preserved. The core identity (aerial superiority leading to disorientation) remains.

**Heartpiercing Strike → Murderous Intent**
- *Soul:* A devastating attack that's hard to resist, with movement on failure
- *Preserved:* High TN check, critical strike, reduced resistance TN, movement on failure
- *Adapted:* Works with any Combat skill, not just Martial Arts
- *Verdict:* Soul preserved. The core identity (a committed, deadly strike) remains.

## Common Conversion Patterns

### Pattern: Ring-Specific Opportunity Spend

**L5R:** "Spend Fire (op) to [effect]"
**GFL5R:** "Spend Power (op) to [effect]"

Map the ring to the appropriate approach based on the technique's flavor.

### Pattern: Void Point Cost

**L5R:** "Spend 1 Void point to [effect]"
**GFL5R:** "Spend 1 Fortune point to [effect]"

Void points become Fortune points. The maximum pool and recovery methods differ, but the spend pattern is the same.

### Pattern: Honor/Glory Cost

**L5R:** "Forfeit X honor to [effect]"
**GFL5R:** Options:
- Remove the cost entirely if it doesn't fit
- Convert to Fortune point cost
- Convert to Humanity loss (for morally questionable techniques)
- Convert to Fame loss (for socially risky techniques)

### Pattern: Stance/Posture

**L5R:** "Prevent them from selecting that ring as their stance"
**GFL5R:** "Prevent them from selecting that approach as their posture"

Stances become postures. Rings become approaches.

### Pattern: Range Bands

**L5R:** Range 0-1, range 2-3, etc.
**GFL5R:** Same system. Range bands are unchanged.

### Pattern: Conditions

Most conditions map directly, but some require adaptation:

| L5R Condition | GFL5R Condition | Notes |
|---------------|-----------------|-------|
| Bleeding | Bleeding | Direct mapping |
| Burning | Burning | Direct mapping |
| Dazed | Dazed | Direct mapping |
| Disoriented | Disoriented | Direct mapping |
| Enraged | Enraged | Direct mapping |
| Intoxicated | Intoxicated | Direct mapping |
| Prone | Prone | Direct mapping |
| Silenced | Silenced | Direct mapping |
| Afflicted | ELID or Parapluie | Depends on character biology: ELID for biological characters exposed to Collapse radiation, Parapluie for T-Dolls/neural cloud entities. Transhumanist characters can suffer both. |
| Compromised | Compromised | Direct mapping |
| Unconscious | Unconscious | Direct mapping |
| Incapacitated | Incapacitated | Direct mapping |
| Immobilized | Immobilized | Direct mapping |

**L5R conditions without direct GFL5R equivalents:**
- **Airborne:** Consider using the movement rules or creating a "Levitating" condition
- **Concealed:** Use Stealth skill mechanics instead of a condition
- **Sensing:** Use Insight checks or Vigilance mechanics instead

**GFL5R-specific conditions to consider:**
- **Frightened:** Forced movement away from fear source
- **Focused/Vigilant:** Temporary Focus/Vigilance shifts

## Example: Full Conversion Walkthrough

### Source: L5R's "Spinning Blades Style"

**Original:**
"When making a Martial Arts [Melee] or Martial Arts [Unarmed] Attack action check with a second readied weapon, spend (op) equal to your target's Vigilance to use that weapon against one target, inflicting physical damage equal to its base damage, or if the target is Dazed, increase this damage by your ranks in the skill used for the check."

### Step 1: Identify
- **Green flags:** Clear mechanical purpose, weapon-agnostic (just needs "second readied weapon"), opportunity spend pattern
- **Verdict:** Good candidate for conversion

### Step 2: Summarize
"When attacking with two weapons, spend opportunities equal to the target's Vigilance to make an extra attack with your off-hand weapon, dealing extra damage if the target is Dazed."

### Step 3: Convert
This is a dual-wielding technique. In a firearms-first world, this becomes dual-wielding pistols. The technique's soul is about the off-hand attack, not the weapon type.

**Converted summary:** "When making a Firearms attack with a second readied weapon, spend opportunities equal to the target's Vigilance to deal additional damage with the second weapon, increased if the target is Dazed."

### Step 4: Un-Summarize

**Name:** Guns Akimbo
**Rank:** 1
**Type:** Combat
**Approach:** null (works with any approach)
**Skill:** Firearms

**Flavor:** "Two guns are better than one. A trained operator can track two targets simultaneously, or pour double the fire into a single target. The key is rhythm: fire, transition, fire, transition."

**Description:**
```
<h3>Activation:</h3>
<p>When making a Firearms Attack action check with a second readied weapon, you may spend (op) as follows:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>(op) equal to target's Vigilance:</strong> Deal additional damage to the target equal to the second weapon's base damage. If the target is Dazed, increase this damage by your ranks in Firearms.</li>
</ul>
```

### Step 5: Verify
- **Soul:** Dual-wielding for extra damage, bonus against Dazed targets
- **Preserved:** Second weapon damage, Vigilance cost, Dazed bonus
- **Adapted:** Melee weapons → firearms
- **Verdict:** Soul preserved. The technique still rewards dual-wielding and capitalizing on Dazed targets.

## Final Checklist

Before submitting a converted technique, verify:

- [ ] Name is evocative and fits GFL5R's tone
- [ ] Rank is appropriate for the power level
- [ ] Type matches the technique's category
- [ ] Approach is correct (or null if approach-agnostic)
- [ ] Skill is correct (or null if skill-agnostic)
- [ ] Flavor text is 1-3 sentences and fits the setting
- [ ] Description uses proper HTML formatting
- [ ] Activation conditions are clear
- [ ] Effects are unambiguous
- [ ] New Opportunities follow the (op) cost pattern
- [ ] Resistance checks include alternate TNs
- [ ] The soul of the original technique is preserved
- [ ] No L5R-specific terminology remains (rings, stances, void points, etc.)
- [ ] The technique fits GFL5R's firearms-first paradigm (or has a good reason to stay melee)
