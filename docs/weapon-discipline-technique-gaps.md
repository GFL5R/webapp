# Weapon Discipline Technique Gaps

Analysis of each weapon discipline's current technique coverage and ideas for new techniques to fill the gaps. Techniques are organized by rank (R1, R2, R3) with pseudo-mechanics describing the general mechanical shape without exact numbers.

---

## Knives

**Skills:** Blades, Stealth, Subterfuge
**Perk:** Sleight of Hand (free draw/stow knife, +deadliness from concealed)
**Capstone:** Death's Whisper (spend Fortune to convert hit into crit = deadliness + bonus successes)

### Current Techniques
| Rank | Techniques |
|------|-----------|
| R1 | Throwing Knife, Silent Strike |
| R2 | Gap in the Armor, Closing Strike, Momentous Strike |
| R3 | *(none)* |

### Gap: R3 Techniques

**Pin Cushion**
- Concept: Rapid successive stabs that overwhelm the target's ability to defend.
- Shape: Attack action with a knife. On success, if you have moved this round, deal additional damage based on the number of range bands traveled. Each additional hit beyond the first increases the TN of the target's Conditioning check to resist the critical strike.
- Why: Knives reward mobility (Closing Strike, Momentous Strike already do this). A rank 3 technique that rewards aggressive knife work and makes the target's defense harder fits the escalation pattern.

**Between the Ribs**
- Concept: A single, perfectly placed thrust that bypasses armor entirely.
- Shape: Attack action with a knife at range 0. Spend (op) to ignore armor equal to (op) spent. If the target is unaware of you or you have the Stealth skill, increase the weapon's deadliness by your ranks in Blades.
- Why: The knife equivalent of Hand of God. A surgical strike that rewards the discipline's stealth theme. Differentiates from Sicario's Killshot by being about precision placement rather than exploiting unaware targets.

## Swords

**Skills:** Blades, Conditioning, Resolve
**Perk:** Razor Edged (inflict bleeding on hit, duration = discipline ranks)
**Capstone:** Living Blade (free counterattack when a melee attack misses you)

### Current Techniques
| Rank | Techniques |
|------|-----------|
| R1 | *(none)* |
| R2 | Acrobatic Recovery |
| R3 | Deflective Defense, Cleaving Strike, Counterattack |

### Gap: R1 Techniques

**Rising Edge**
- Concept: A basic upward slash that catches opponents off guard, especially those who are prone or recovering.
- Shape: Attack action with a sword. If your target is Prone or Dazed, reduce the TN of the attack. On success, the target must resist or be pushed 1 range band away.
- Why: Swords need a foundational R1 technique. This is simple, reliable sword work that rewards reading the battlefield. Synergizes with the Conditioning skill (knowing when to strike a recovering opponent).

**Press the Advantage**
- Concept: Maintain relentless offensive pressure so the target can't recover.
- Shape: When you succeed on an Attack action with a sword, spend (op) to reduce the TN of your next Attack action with a sword this round by 1 per (op) spent. If your next attack also succeeds, you may continue the chain.
- Why: A basic R1 technique that teaches the sword's offensive identity. Simple opportunity spend that rewards aggressive play. Fits the "versatile weapon for offense and defense" flavor.

### Gap: Additional R2 Techniques

**Bind and Strike**
- Concept: Lock blades with the opponent, then use the leverage to create an opening.
- Shape: When you are targeted by a melee Attack action and have a readied sword, spend (op) to reduce the incoming attack's successes. If you reduce it enough to cause a failure, you may immediately make an Attack action against the attacker.
- Why: Bridges the gap between R1 offense and R3 Deflective Defense. A mid-tier defensive technique that's more nuanced than just "take less damage." Sword-specific because it requires a blade to bind with.

## Pistols

**Skills:** Firearms, Subterfuge, Tactics
**Perk:** Quick Draw (free draw/stow, reroll dice when attacking with just-drawn pistol)
**Capstone:** Deadeye (called shot: Disarm, Cripple, or Disable on hit)

### Current Techniques
| Rank | Techniques |
|------|-----------|
| R1 | Sighting-in, Shoot to Maim |
| R2 | Guns Akimbo, Disarming Shot |
| R3 | *(none)* |

### Gap: R3 Techniques

**Hammer the Anvil**
- Concept: Fire rapidly to keep the target pinned, then deliver a single precise shot when they're committed to cover.
- Shape: Once per scene, as an Attack action with a pistol, make a Firearms check. If you succeed, your target suffers the normal damage. Then, spend (op) to make an immediate second Attack action against the same target. If the second attack hits, increase its deadliness by the number of (op) spent.
- Why: The pistol capstone (Deadeye) is about precision. The R3 technique should complement it by being about sustained pistol fire, showing the other side of pistol mastery. "Hammer the anvil" = suppress then strike.

**Point Blank Execution**
- Concept: Press the pistol directly against the target and fire. No missing, but risky positioning.
- Shape: As an Attack and Movement action with a pistol at range 0, make a TN X Firearms check. If you succeed, the attack cannot be dodged or deflected (the target must resist with Conditioning only, no approach choice). If you fail, you suffer the Dazed condition from the target's desperate close-quarters resistance.
- Why: High risk, high reward. Very pistol. Fits the Subterfuge skill (getting that close without being noticed) and the Deadeye capstone (guaranteed hit if you land it).

## Submachine Guns

**Skills:** Firearms, Stealth, Tactics
**Perk:** Room Presence (hit additional targets at range 0-2 of primary, up to discipline ranks)
**Capstone:** Bullet Storm (spend Fortune for free second Attack action at range 0-2)

### Current Techniques
| Rank | Techniques
|------|-----------|
| R1 | Aggressive Escort |
| R2 | Acrobatic Recovery, Distraction Tactics |
| R3 | *(none)* |

### Gap: R3 Techniques

**Walking Fire**
- Concept: Advance while firing, using the SMG's maneuverability to pressure multiple positions.
- Shape: As an Attack and Movement action with an SMG, make a Firearms check. If you succeed, deal damage to your target and you may move 1 range band. Spend (op) to move 1 additional range band per (op) spent. Each character you pass within range 0-1 of during this movement must resist or suffer the Dazed condition.
- Why: SMGs are about close-quarters dominance and mobility. This technique lets you clear a room by advancing through it, suppressing everyone you pass. Fits the "aggressive posture" flavor.

**Spray and Pray**
- Concept: Fire blindly into an area to deny movement and force enemies to keep their heads down.
- Shape: As an Attack action with an SMG, choose a position at range 0-3. All characters within range 0-1 of that position must resist or suffer the Dazed condition and receive strife. You do not need line of sight to the position, but you must know its location.
- Why: Area denial is core to SMG doctrine. This technique fills the suppression niche at R3, complementing the Room Presence perk (which is about hitting multiple targets) with a technique about controlling space.

### Gap: Additional R1 Technique

**Corner Slice**
- Concept: Quick exposure around cover to fire a burst, then immediately pull back.
- Shape: When you are in cover and make an Attack action with an SMG, spend (op) to gain a bonus to your physical resistance against the next Attack action that targets you before the end of your next turn. If you are in Obscuring or Entangling terrain, increase this bonus.
- Why: SMGs excel in urban CQB where corners and cover are everywhere. A foundational technique that teaches the SMG's hit-and-run identity.

## Shotguns

**Skills:** Firearms, Conditioning, Survival
**Perk:** Point Blank (+damage and +deadliness at range 1 or lower, scaling with discipline ranks)
**Capstone:** Thunderclap (kill a target, nearby enemies must Resolve check or become Frightened)

### Current Techniques
| Rank | Techniques |
|------|-----------|
| R1 | Pistol Whip |
| R2 | Crushing Blow |
| R3 | *(none)* |

### Gap: R3 Techniques

**Door Breacher**
- Concept: Use the shotgun to blow through barriers, doors, and cover, then follow up.
- Shape: As a Movement and Attack action, make a TN X Firearms check targeting a barrier, door, or piece of cover at range 0-1. If you succeed, the barrier is destroyed (or gains the Destroyed quality if it was already Damaged). Characters on the other side must resist or suffer the Dazed condition and physical damage. You may immediately move through the destroyed barrier.
- Why: Shotguns are the premier breaching tool. This is a combat technique that turns the environment into an advantage. Fits the Conditioning skill (physically moving through the breach) and Survival (improvised problem-solving).

**Scatter Shot**
- Concept: Widen the spread to hit everything in a cone, sacrificing single-target damage for area coverage.
- Shape: As an Attack action with a shotgun, choose a cone direction. All characters within range 0-2 in that direction must resist or suffer physical damage. Reduce the weapon's base damage and increase the TN, but the attack cannot miss entirely (minimum 1 success on the check deals half damage to all targets).
- Why: The shotgun's spread is its defining feature. This technique lets the shotgun user embrace the spread for crowd control, complementing Point Blank (single-target devastation) with an area option.

### Gap: Additional R1 Technique

**Rack and Ready**
- Concept: The distinctive sound of a shotgun being racked is itself a weapon.
- Shape: Once per scene as a Support action, make a TN X Command or Intimidation check. Each character within earshot who can hear the rack must resist or receive strife. Characters who fail also treat their vigilance as lower for the purpose of detecting you until the end of your next turn.
- Why: The shotgun rack is iconic. A foundational technique that uses the weapon's psychological presence. Fits the Survival skill (understanding how to use the environment and psychology to your advantage).

## Assault Rifles

**Skills:** Firearms, Tactics, Conditioning
**Perk:** Versatile (reduce TN penalty for attacking outside ideal range)
**Capstone:** Lead Rain (downtime: apply one weapon quality to your assault rifle)

### Current Techniques
| Rank | Techniques |
|------|-----------|
| R1 | Sighting-in |
| R2 | Airborne Assault, Anchor Point, Eyes on the Prize |
| R3 | *(none)* |

### Gap: R3 Techniques

**Controlled Burst**
- Concept: Fire a precise three-round burst that balances accuracy and lethality.
- Shape: As an Attack action with an assault rifle, choose to fire a controlled burst. Increase the weapon's deadliness. If you succeed, you may spend (op) to inflict a condition on the target (Dazed if 1 (op), Disoriented if 2 (op)). If you fail, you suffer strife from the wasted ammunition.
- Why: The assault rifle's signature firing mode. A technique that rewards precision with the platform's standard capability. Bridges the gap between the versatile perk (range flexibility) and the Lead Rain capstone (customization).

**Suppression Fire**
- Concept: Lay down continuous fire to keep enemies pinned and unable to move.
- Shape: As an Attack action with an assault rifle, choose a position at range 0-4. All characters within range 0-1 of that position must resist or suffer strife and be unable to move from their current position until the end of their next turn. You may maintain this effect by spending your action each round to continue firing.
- Why: Suppression is the assault rifle's most important tactical role. This differentiates from the Machine Gun's Suppressive Instinct perk by being a technique rather than a passive, and from Anchor Point by being about area denial rather than drawing fire to yourself.

### Gap: Additional R1 Technique

**Ready Position**
- Concept: Maintain constant readiness to engage targets at any range.
- Shape: When you perform a Movement action with a readied assault rifle, spend (op) to reduce the TN of the next Attack action you make with that rifle this round. If you did not move, you may instead spend (op) to add bonus dice to your next Attack action.
- Why: The assault rifle is "never the worst choice." A foundational technique that rewards the rifleman's adaptability. Simple, flexible, and teaches the assault rifle's identity as the reliable all-rounder.

## Battle Rifles

**Skills:** Firearms, Resolve, Tactics
**Perk:** Heavy Hitter (ignore armor equal to discipline ranks)
**Capstone:** Stopping Power (ignore cover TN bonuses and damage reduction, overpenetrate to target behind)

### Current Techniques
| Rank | Techniques |
|------|-----------|
| R1 | Eyes Up!, Sighting-in |
| R2 | *(none)* |
| R3 | Hand of God |

### Gap: R2 Techniques

**Recoil Management**
- Concept: A skilled battle rifle shooter can compensate for the heavy recoil through technique and body positioning.
- Shape: When you make an Attack action with a battle rifle, spend (op) to reduce the TN penalty for attacking outside your weapon's ideal range. If you succeed, you may spend additional (op) to increase the weapon's damage based on (op) spent, representing follow-up shots that land because you controlled the recoil.
- Why: Battle rifles have "increased recoil" in their flavor text. A technique that directly addresses this weakness and turns it into a strength is thematically perfect. Fits Resolve (mental discipline to control the weapon) and Tactics (knowing when to push through recoil vs. take careful shots).

**Magnetic Drift**
- Concept: Fire a shot that seems to miss, but the heavy round's trajectory carries it into a secondary target.
- Shape: When you fail an Attack action with a battle rifle, spend (op). If you do, choose a character within range 0-2 of your original target. That character must resist or suffer physical damage equal to half the weapon's base damage (rounded down). This damage ignores armor.
- Why: Battle rifle rounds are powerful enough to threaten even on a miss. This technique turns misses into near-hits, fitting the "stopping power" theme. It's a safety net that rewards the high-risk, high-reward nature of the weapon.

### Gap: Additional R1 Technique

**Braced Shot**
- Concept: Take a moment to stabilize the rifle against cover or the ground for a more accurate shot.
- Shape: As a Support and Attack action with a battle rifle, if you are prone, in cover, or braced against a solid object, make a Firearms check with reduced TN. If you succeed, increase the weapon's deadliness. You cannot move this round.
- Why: Battle rifles reward patience and positioning. A foundational technique that teaches the battle rifle's identity as a marksman's weapon that trades mobility for power. Fits Resolve (patience) and the Heavy Hitter perk (armor penetration on a stable platform).

## Snipers

**Skills:** Firearms, Stealth, Survival
**Perk:** Patience (reroll dice equal to discipline ranks if you haven't moved)
**Capstone:** One Shot (increase TN by 2, on hit: crit with severity 5 + deadliness + bonus successes)

### Current Techniques
| Rank | Techniques |
|------|-----------|
| R1 | Sighting-in |
| R2 | Battle in the Mind |
| R3 | Sniper's Advantage, Hand of God |

### Gap: Additional R1 Technique

**Spotter's Mark**
- Concept: Identify a target's weak point and communicate it to allies.
- Shape: As a Support action, make a TN X Insight or Survival check targeting one character within your weapon's range. If you succeed, choose one ally. That ally gains a bonus to their next Attack action against the target. You may spend (op) to extend this bonus to additional allies.
- Why: Snipers don't work alone. A foundational technique that uses the Survival skill (reading the environment) and supports the team. Differentiates from Sighting-in (which is about range) by being about target identification.

### Gap: Additional R2 Technique

**High Ground**
- Concept: Elevation advantage is everything for a sniper. Higher ground means better sightlines and harder return fire.
- Shape: As a Movement and Support action, if you move to a position that is elevated above your targets, make a TN X Survival or Stealth check. If you succeed, increase the range of your readied sniper rifle and reduce the TN of your next Attack action. You may spend (op) to become concealed from characters below you.
- Why: Snipers are defined by their positioning. This technique rewards the Stealth and Survival skills (finding and reaching good positions) and creates a meaningful tactical choice about movement.

## Machine Guns

**Skills:** Firearms, Conditioning, Resolve
**Perk:** Suppressive Instinct (spend (op) on hit: nearby enemies choose between strife or forfeit movement)
**Capstone:** Wall of Lead (spend (op) to convert into bonus damage, up to discipline ranks)

### Current Techniques
| Rank | Techniques |
|------|-----------|
| R1 | *(none)* |
| R2 | Anchor Point, Landmine, Last Stand |
| R3 | *(none)* |

### Gap: R1 Techniques

**Set Up**
- Concept: Machine guns require time to deploy effectively. Setting up a bipod or finding a stable firing position dramatically improves performance.
- Shape: As a Support action, make a TN X Conditioning check. If you succeed, you set up your machine gun in a stable position. While set up, increase the weapon's damage and reduce the TN of Attack actions made with it. You cannot move from your position without losing this benefit (requiring another Support action to re-establish).
- Why: The foundational machine gun technique. MGs are positional weapons. This teaches the core identity: find a good spot, dig in, and dominate. Fits Conditioning (physical effort of setting up) and Resolve (patience to hold position).

**Beaten Zone**
- Concept: The area where a machine gun's bullets naturally fall due to recoil and spread. A skilled gunner can use this to their advantage.
- Shape: When you make an Attack action with a machine gun, spend (op) to define a beaten zone (a position at range 0-3). Characters within range 0-1 of that position who move must resist or suffer physical damage. The beaten zone persists until the end of your next turn.
- Why: "Beaten zone" is a real machine gun concept. This technique teaches area denial at R1, establishing the MG's role as a battlefield control weapon before the more advanced techniques at R2.

### Gap: R3 Techniques

**Final Protective Fire**
- Concept: When everything goes wrong and the enemy is about to overrun your position, you dump everything you have into them.
- Shape: Once per scene, when you or an ally within range 0-2 would suffer a critical strike, you may immediately make an Attack action with your machine gun targeting any number of characters within range 0-2. The TN is reduced. Each target must resist or suffer physical damage and receive strife. After resolving this technique, your weapon gains the Damaged quality.
- Why: "Final protective fire" is a real military doctrine. The machine gunner's last resort. This is a dramatic R3 technique that fits the Resolve skill (holding the line when things are worst) and the Wall of Lead capstone (maximum (op) conversion into damage).

**Walking the Burst**
- Concept: Adjust fire mid-burst to track a moving target or sweep across multiple targets.
- Shape: When you make an Attack action with a machine gun and succeed, spend (op) to shift your fire to an additional target within range 0-1 of the original target per (op) spent. Each additional target suffers damage equal to the weapon's base damage (no bonus successes applied to them).
- Why: Machine guns can engage multiple targets by adjusting aim. This complements the Suppressive Instinct perk (which affects targets near your primary) by letting you directly hit multiple targets. Fits the "overwhelming firepower" identity.
