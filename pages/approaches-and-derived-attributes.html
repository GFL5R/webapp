Approaches

Every character has five Approaches. These are your core stats, representing both your ability and your style when facing challenges. A higher Approach means you roll more dice when using it, but the Approach you choose also changes how you narrate your action.

Fortune

Instinct, intuition, and improbable insight. Characters act through gut feeling, sudden clarity, or fate bending outcomes in their favor.

Power

The force that drives direct action. Intensity, decisive aggression, emotional momentum, and the strength behind strikes, arguments, or bold decisions.

Precision

Careful focus and refined control. Exact timing, measured technique, and a deliberate approach to overcome obstacles with minimal waste.

Resilience

Inner stability and physical endurance. Patience, discipline, and the capacity to withstand pressure, maintain form, and push forward despite setbacks.

Swiftness

Adaptable movement and fast decision-making. Reacting fluidly to changing circumstances, acting with speed, flexibility, and efficient momentum.

Approach Limit

No single Approach may exceed the combined value of a character's two lowest Approaches. This ensures that each character maintains a balanced foundation and cannot specialize beyond what their overall capability supports.

◇ EXAMPLE: APPROACHES IN ACTION

The squad reaches a locked security door deep inside a collapsed research facility. Time is short.

Using Power

The first character squares their stance and grips the doorframe. Their muscles tense as they draw on raw force. With a single hard pull, the hinges bend. A second shove sends the door crashing inward. The barrier gives way through strength and momentum.

Using Precision

The second character kneels beside the lock and studies its internals. They withdraw a compact tool kit and work with steady, exact motions. Each pin clicks into place under careful pressure. After a quiet moment of focused adjustment, the mechanism releases. The door swings open cleanly, leaving no trace of entry.

Derived Attributes

Derived Attributes are secondary statistics calculated from a character's Approaches. Unlike Approaches or Skills, players do not assign points to these directly. Each Derived Attribute reflects how a character performs under pressure, manages long tasks, or reacts to threats.

Endurance

Physical stamina and ability to keep moving through pain or exhaustion.

(Power + Resilience) × 2

Composure

Ability to stay calm, maintain emotional balance during stressful moments.

(Resilience + Swiftness) × 2

Vigilance

Awareness of shifting conditions, hidden threats, or subtle movement.

(Precision + Swiftness) ÷ 2 ↑

Focus

Ability to stay on task and work through long or repetitive actions.

Power + Precision

Fortune Points

Fortune Points are a renewable resource that allows characters to bend events toward a favorable outcome.

A character begins play with Fortune Points equal to half of their Fortune (rounded down), and their maximum pool is equal to their full Fortune.

Spending Fortune Points

Fortune Points may be spent in situations such as:

  • Increasing your dice pool by 1 for a single check
  • Enduring an event that would otherwise incapacitate or destroy the character
  • Activating techniques or abilities that require a Fortune Point

Gaining Fortune Points

Characters regain Fortune Points by encountering meaningful adversity. This includes:

  • Failing a check where a disadvantage is relevant
  • Once per scene when an anxiety meaningfully affects the situation
  • Whenever the GM conceals the TN of a check from the player, creating uncertainty or risk

Approach Ranks

1
Limited
Limited capability. Often defers to others or relies on different methods.
2
Typical
Functions at a typical level. Handles most tasks, though performance rarely stands out.
3
Strong
Shows clear strength. Others recognize the confidence and consistency.
4
Remarkable
Demonstrates remarkable control. Reflects training, insight, or natural ability beyond the norm.
5
Mastery
Embodies this Approach with uncommon mastery. Defines expectations for the style.
6
Beyond Human
Capability associated with elite T-Dolls, advanced prototypes, or entities shaped by forces outside normal experience. Outside the range of standard character creation.
