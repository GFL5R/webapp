Building a Commander

Every Commander in this game begins in the same place: working as a licensed bounty hunter under B.R.I.E.F. The work is dangerous, often thankless, but it pays in credits, gear, and reputation. This chapter guides you step by step through creating your Commander—showing how you ended up here and what sets you apart.

Question 1. Where are you from?

In 2070, most surviving Green Zones are nominally under the URNC umbrella. But most people still hold onto the idea of nationality. Where you were born shapes how others see you, and how you see the world.

Every Commander begins with all Approaches at 1. Your nationality grants +1 to two Approaches.

Your nationality defines where you were born, but your background defines what you did before you became a bounty hunter.

Your background grants +1 to one Approach and one starting Skill. Feel free to come up with your own!

Question 3. What path have you chosen?

What kind of Commander are you? Some fight shoulder-to-shoulder with their Dolls, others coordinate from the rear, negotiate with allies, or outthink their rivals.

Select one Discipline. It is now unlocked; you gain its starting skills and perk.

Question 4. How do you stand out in your field?

Maybe you have a talent for keeping calm when everything goes wrong. Maybe you're unusually lucky, or skilled at reading people. Or maybe you've simply refused to quit when others broke.

Select one Advantage from the list of advantages.

Question 5. What holds you back the most?

You've survived this long, but survival doesn't mean perfection. Maybe you carry a bad habit, a scar from your past, or a weakness you can't shake.

Select one Disadvantage from the list of disadvantages.

Question 6. What sets you apart from other Humans?

Maybe you have a ritual before every mission, a superstition you refuse to let go, or a hobby that makes no sense to anyone else. It doesn't have to make sense. It just has to be yours.

Select one Passion from the list of passions.

Question 7. What do you fear the most?

Even hardened veterans carry fear. Maybe it's failing your squad. Maybe it's losing control, or being powerless when people depend on you.

Select one Anxiety from the list of anxieties.

Question 8. How do you view Dolls?

Every Commander works with Dolls, but not every Commander sees them the same way. Your answer shapes how your Doll experiences their bond with you. Respect, suspicion, affection, or distance—whichever you choose will color every interaction.

Decide how your Commander views Dolls. Are they tools? Allies? Students? Friends? Experiments? Something else entirely?

Question 9. What do you fight for?

Some chase money, fame, or power. Others fight for ideals: to protect people, rebuild the world, or carve out a place where their name will be remembered. A few are simply running from something they'd rather not face.

Choose a personal goal. This is what your Commander wants to achieve—even at the cost of everyone around you.

Question 10. What does your name mean?

A name is never just a label. Maybe it carries family history, a call sign earned in the field, or a name you chose when the old life didn't fit anymore. Names shape expectations in introductions, contracts, and whispers behind your back.

Decide what your name means. Was it inherited, gifted, earned, or taken?

Question 11. What is your name?

Write it down. Whatever you choose, this is who you are to the world—the name your Doll will call in the heat of battle, and the one people will remember when you're gone.

Question 12. How will your story end?

Everyone dies. Some quietly, some suddenly, some in ways that change the world around them. Maybe you expect to go out in battle, side by side with your Doll. Maybe you imagine living long enough to see the world rebuilt.

This question has no bearing on mechanics, but keep your answer in mind. Your GM certainly will…
