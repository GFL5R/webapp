Building Your T-Doll

T-Dolls and Gender

Typically, T-Dolls are most often female-presenting. However, since you're creating your own here, there's no reason yours has to be!

Creating a T-Doll is different from creating your Commander. Humans are shaped by their pasts: choices, training, and circumstance. Dolls are built. Every model starts with a frame—the physical chassis that defines baseline strengths and limitations. From there, your Doll can be fitted with upgrades, modules, and quirks that make them more than just another unit off the production line.

But make no mistake: Dolls are more than the sum of their parts. They may have been assembled in a factory, but they grow, change, and express themselves in ways no design document could predict.

Question 1. Who made you, and what is your model number?

Every Doll begins with a Frame: the factory that produced you and the model designation stamped into your chassis. Your Frame sets your baseline Approaches, gives you two starting Skills, and anchors roleplaying flavor.

Question 2. What weapon were you imprinted with?

From the moment of activation, most T-Dolls carry a weapon imprint. For some, this imprint feels like destiny. For others, it's a shackle—an expectation they've struggled to outgrow.

Select a weapon discipline from the list. You begin play with the first rank completed.

Question 3. What upgrades set you apart?

Every unit of your model line rolled off with the same baseline specs. But you aren't just another number in a production log. Somewhere along the way, someone invested in you.

10 Upgrade Points

Spend them in three ways:

  • Increase an Approach (2 points): Raise one Approach by +1. (Max 4 at creation.)
  • Train a Skill (1 point): Add one Skill to your repertoire.
  • Install an Upgrade Module (cost varies): Select modules from the list of modules.

Question 4. What is your greatest achievement so far?

At some point, you went beyond simply performing: you excelled. Perhaps you carried a mission under impossible odds, protected someone who mattered, or solved a problem no one expected.

Select one Advantage from the list of advantages.

Question 5. What holds you back most?

No Doll is flawless. Some carry limitations hardwired into their frames; others bear damage or errors that linger in their systems.

Select one Disadvantage from the list of disadvantages.

Question 6. What makes you different?

Every Doll picks up quirks: odd habits, fascinations, or interests that weren't part of the blueprint. These make you stand out, and they're part of your identity.

Select one Passion from the list of passions.

Question 7. What are you most afraid of?

Even the strongest frame can hesitate. Deep in your neural cloud, there's something that gnaws at you: a thought you can't banish, a risk you dread, or a future you don't want to face.

Select one Anxiety from the list of anxieties.

Question 8. When did you meet your Commander?

For some, it begins the moment they're activated—stepping off the assembly line to salute the human they were made to serve. For others, it's more complicated: reassigned after a failed mission, transferred between owners, or pulled from a scrapyard and given a second chance.

Describe the circumstances of your first meeting. This moment sets the tone for your partnership and may still color how you see each other today.

Question 9. What do you want for yourself?

No matter how kind a Commander, how close the bond—they are still not you. Your missions may give you purpose, but you carry a purpose of your own as well.

Choose a personal goal. This is the horizon your Doll is always walking toward, whether in secret or in plain sight.

Question 10. How were you named?

Every Doll receives a designation, but names are rarely so simple. A name carries weight—it shapes how humans see you, how other Dolls relate to you, and how you think about yourself.

Question 11. What is your name?

Whatever you choose, this is the name the world knows you by, and the one you'll answer to in play. Write it down on your sheet.

Question 12. How does your story end?

Every Doll wonders, in quiet moments, how long they have left. Some imagine safe retirement. Others see themselves scrapped on a workbench. Many expect to die in battle, protecting a Commander, a squadmate, or a mission that mattered more than they did.

This question has no bearing on mechanics, but keep your answer in mind. Your GM certainly will…
