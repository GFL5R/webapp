Collapse Radiation

Collapse Fluid & Radiation

Collapse Fluid is an exotic state of matter engineered to disrupt the attractive and repulsive forces between particles. It propagates as a particle-wave through matter until it reaches quantum stability, at which point its expansion halts.

When Collapse Fluid permeates matter, it emits Collapse radiation. This radiation destabilizes biological and electronic systems alike. In organic tissue, it induces aggressive cancerous growth and neurological failure. In electronics, it corrupts data integrity, sensor coherence, and long-term system stability.

Collapse radiation does not dissipate harmlessly. Exposure accumulates. Survival is measured not by avoidance, but by how long degradation can be managed.

Effects on Humans

High-dose exposure produces rapid, catastrophic failure: coughing blood, severe head and abdominal pain, vision loss, convulsions, fatigue, scorch-like lesions, and periods of unconsciousness. Extreme exposure is typically fatal within minutes or hours.

Lower doses accumulated over time produce ELID (Eurosky Low-Emission Infectious Disease): a progressive condition marked by neurological degradation, escalating aggression, and severe physical mutation.

Effects on T-Dolls

T-Dolls do not develop ELID. However, Collapse radiation interferes with electronics: memory corruption, hallucinations, and neural cloud instability. At extreme levels, full system shutdown or irreversible data loss.

Example: Exposure at a Blast Site

A Collapse Bomb detonates near a rail spur. You sprint for cover as the shockwave passes. Your T-Doll catches you as you cough blood and stumble. Moments later, she looks at you and asks, "Wait, who are you, again?"

Collapse Exposure Rules

Collapse exposure is cumulative and persistent. It does not fade with rest, time, or narrative relief. It is tracked explicitly.

The Collapse Track

Capacity

(Sum of all Approaches) × 5

Collapse Value

Starts at 0

Persistence

Never decreases naturally

Reset Condition

Only resets when it fills completely

Gaining Collapse by Zone

ZoneCollapse Gain
GreenNone
Purification+1 per month
Yellow+1 per day
Red+1 per hour
Black+1 per round (6 seconds)

Protective Gear

Protective equipment reduces effective exposure severity by one zone step:

  • Black counts as Red
  • Red counts as Yellow
  • Yellow counts as Green

Zone downgrades do not stack unless explicitly stated. If characters have different protection levels, track effective zones separately.

Example

A Commander with Approaches totaling 15 has a Collapse Track Capacity of 75. They spend 12 hours operating in a Red Zone while fully protected, treating it as Yellow. They gain +1 Collapse. Unprotected, the same time would result in +12 Collapse.

Consequences of Track Completion

When a Collapse Track fills, resolve immediately:

  1. Apply the next ELID stage Condition if the character is human.
  2. Reset the Collapse Track to 0.
  3. Begin or continue ELID progression.

ELID

ELID (Eurosky Low-Emission Infectious Disease) is the chronic consequence of Collapse radiation: a slow, cumulative rewiring of living tissue that hardens muscles and vessels, erodes higher cognition into feral aggression, and eventually transforms survivors into hostile infected. Tissues silicify and jagged crystal growths erupt along bones, joints, and major organs.

ELID Stages

Each stage is defined as a Condition (see Conditions):

  • Early-Stage ELID
  • Middle-Stage ELID
  • Late-Stage ELID

ELID Progression

  • Progression is checked weekly.
  • Each stage uses the same Resolve check structure.
  • The TN is based on weeks since last effective treatment, not total time since infection.

Example

A character contracts Early-Stage ELID. Week 1 passes with no treatment. In Week 2 they receive a vaccine. In Week 3, the Resolve check uses TN 1, not TN 3.

Treatment & Access

Vaccines

  • Effect: Resets the progression timer for the current ELID stage.
  • Limit: Does not reduce ELID severity.
  • Restriction: Ineffective at Late-Stage ELID.

Surgical Intervention

  • Effect: Reduces ELID severity by one stage (Early removes ELID entirely; Middle reduces to Early; Late reduces to Middle).
  • Limit: Does not alter Collapse exposure history or track behavior.

Access to surgery is a strategic asset. The GM should treat it as leverage, not as routine care.

Containment Zones

Civil authorities classify territory by Collapse radiation density and environmental stability:

White Zone

Radiation is functionally non-detectable. Infrastructure supports long-term human life without protective equipment.

Near-utopian enclaves resembling pre-war life: advanced medicine, cultural continuity, public infrastructure that does not feel temporary. However, access is not a right—entry is purchased through wealth, service, or extreme value extraction. Citizenship is transactional. Scarcity is intentional.

Green Zone

Low, stable radiation profiles with routine monitoring confirming levels below clinical thresholds.

Technically safe and functionally oppressive. Dense, surveilled, and economically sharp-edged—quality of life closer to a neon-lit austerity city than a reconstruction utopia. Green Zones remain stable because the desperate self-select out into danger zones.

Yellow Zone

Wastelands: barren deserts, salt flats, or skeletal ruins. Radiation is moderate and variable.

  • Long-term habitation is strongly discouraged and rarely sanctioned.
  • Human activity requires protective equipment, dose tracking, and evacuation plans.
  • T-Dolls operate safely under normal parameters but require periodic diagnostics.
  • Access is licensed, monitored, and often contingent on corporate or military contracts.

Purification Zone

Between Yellow and Green classifications, under active remediation.

  • Radiation trends downward but fluctuates locally.
  • Habitation is transient and restricted to authorized personnel.
  • Protective requirements vary by site protocol.

Red Zone

Cities and industrial belts that absorbed direct Collapse bomb detonations. Radiation is high and unstable.

  • No permanent habitation beyond heavily shielded research or containment sites.
  • Human presence requires full protective suits, strict time limits, and extraction readiness.
  • T-Dolls can operate for days but accumulate system faults and corrupted logs.
  • Access is mission-specific, tightly controlled, and often classified.

Black Zone (Ground Zero)

Places where reality never finished breaking. Near-constant Collapse storm with luminous dust, static-laced fog, or airborne crystal shards. The ground erupts in jagged crystalline growths.

  • Radiation levels are extreme, volatile, and continuous.
  • Survival without treatment is measured in minutes, even with protection.
  • Terrain is vitrified, structurally unstable, and subject to spontaneous crystal surges.
  • T-Dolls suffer rapid disablement without heavy hardening and frequent resets.
  • Entry is prohibited except under sovereign directive or extraordinary emergency.

Collapse Storms

Collapse storms are transient surges in radiation intensity: violent weather fronts that race along transit lines, flare across ruined industrial belts, or roll out of Black Zone horizons.

Onset

Minutes to tens of minutes, often with little warning

Duration

30 to 120 minutes, occasionally longer in heavily contaminated regions

Footprint

Blocks to districts (urban) or whole valleys/coastal shelves (open terrain)

Storm Exposure Rule

During a Collapse storm, treat the affected area as one zone higher for exposure purposes.

Standard protocol is immediate shelter, suspension of nonessential activity, and rapid extraction once conditions stabilize or a secure corridor is verified.
