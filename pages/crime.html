Crime

Criminal activity in 2072 leaves marks. URNC surveillance, corporate logging systems, and private security networks make it difficult to act without generating a record; every intrusion, transaction, and violent act creates data, and data creates evidence.

Evidence

Evidence is the material record of a crime: physical traces, digital logs, documents, and testimony. The GM maintains the evidence picture for any active investigation; players track it when building a case.

Types of Evidence

Physical Evidence

Objects, biometrics, and environmental traces. Spent casings, blood, forced locks, displaced furniture, neural activity logs.

Digital Evidence

Surveillance footage, network access logs, financial records, communications intercepts. The most abundant category in 2072; also the most easily falsified or erased.

Testimonial Evidence

Witness accounts, informant reports, coerced or voluntary statements.

Documentary Evidence

Identity records, permits, manifests, paperwork authentic or forged.

Supporting & Contrary Evidence

Each piece of evidence is either Supporting (points toward the accused) or Contrary (points away). The two types cancel each other out. The net count (Supporting minus Contrary) modifies the TN of accusation checks.

Example

An investigator has three supporting pieces (surveillance log, recovered weapon, matched biometric) and one contrary piece (timestamped entry record placing the suspect elsewhere). Net is +2 Supporting, so the accusation check TN is reduced by 2.

Evidence can be forged, destroyed, or planted. A forged contrary piece counts the same as a genuine one; investigators who later identify the forgery remove it from the picture.

Investigation Checks

Gathering Evidence

Each success on an investigation check produces one piece of evidence. The GM determines whether it is Supporting or Contrary based on what it actually shows.

Opportunities on investigation checks can surface additional details: exact timestamps, financial connections, or specific tools used to alter a document.

Covering Tracks

Characters hiding their involvement make checks to raise the TN of follow-up investigation against that source. The cover check is made at the time of the act.

ActivitySkill
Remove physical traces from a sceneStealth or Subterfuge
Sanitize a location or objectSubterfuge or Science
Delete or corrupt digital recordsComputers
Forge supporting documentation or evidenceDeception or Subterfuge
Provide a false account under questioningDeception

A successful cover check raises investigation TN by 1. Success with 2+ bonus successes raises it by 2 instead. Failure means the source can be investigated at standard TN.

Accusation Checks

When someone formally or effectively accuses a character of a crime, they make an Accusation Check.

Base TN

4

Supporting Evidence

Reduce TN by 1 per net piece

Contrary Evidence

Increase TN by 1 per net piece

The check uses:

  • Negotiation if presenting a truthful case.
  • Deception if lying, framing, or misrepresenting evidence.

The accused may contest as direct opposition using Negotiation (truthful defense) or Deception (false cover). Handle as a brief Intrigue for extended confrontation, or a single opposed check for quick resolution.

Example

A corporate investigator has 4 Supporting pieces and 1 Contrary piece. Net is +3 Supporting, so TN drops to 1. Even a weak roll likely succeeds. The Commander's player contests with Deception, hoping to invalidate one piece through cross-examination.

Investigations Against the Party

When NPCs investigate the party, the GM runs it as a background process. At intervals (typically after significant acts or during Downtime scenes), the GM makes investigation checks against the relevant cover TNs.

The party is generally unaware of the case building against them unless they take steps to find out, or until consequences become visible: a tail, frozen accounts, an unexpected summons.

Investigating NPCs

The same system applies when the party builds a case against a target. The GM determines what evidence exists before play; what the party finds depends on where they look and how checks resolve.
