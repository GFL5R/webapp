Disciplines

A discipline is a focused track of training that teaches an operator a specific skillset. Over the course of a campaign, a character may pursue up to five disciplines, building a layered expertise that reflects their growth, priorities, and field experience.

Every character begins play with one discipline. Humans may select any discipline. T-Dolls must select one of the weapon disciplines but begin with Rank 1 already completed, entering play at Rank 2 in that discipline. Humans begin with only the discipline's perk and a single starting technique.

Structure

Each discipline includes:

  • Associated Skills: A set of skills tied to the discipline. XP spent on these skills also counts toward discipline progression.
  • Perk: A passive benefit unlocked immediately when the discipline is added.
  • Techniques: Specialized abilities organized by rank. Each technique is restricted to Rank 1, 2, or 3.
  • Three Ranks: Milestones that unlock access to higher-rank techniques and culminate in a capstone.
  • Capstone: A reward granted upon completing Rank 3.

Progression

Discipline progression is driven by XP spent on the discipline's associated skills and techniques. There is no separate XP pool; spending XP on a relevant skill or technique advances the discipline automatically.

If a character spends XP on a skill before selecting a discipline that lists it, that XP still counts retroactively toward the discipline's progression once it is added.

Adding a Discipline

Picking up a new discipline costs 5 XP. This immediately grants the discipline's perk and counts towards that discipline's first rank. The character may then begin spending XP on its associated skills and techniques to progress through its ranks.

A character may hold up to five disciplines at any time.

Techniques

Each discipline offers a selection of techniques across its three ranks. A character may learn any technique from a rank they are currently working on or have already completed. Beginning to accumulate XP toward a rank counts as working on it; completing the previous rank is not required to access techniques of the next.

Each technique costs 3 XP to learn. This XP also counts toward the discipline's progression, just like skill XP.

Fortification

A character's Fortification value is the sum of all completed ranks across all of their disciplines. Only fully completed ranks count; a rank that is in progress contributes nothing.

A character with two disciplines, one at Rank 2 (working on Rank 2, Rank 1 completed) and one at Rank 1 (working on Rank 1, no ranks completed), has a Fortification value of 1. Completing Rank 2 in the first discipline raises it to 2.

A character with five disciplines all at Rank 1 but none completed has a Fortification value of 0.

Fortification uses a sum rather than an average so that picking up a new discipline never reduces the value. The theoretical maximum Fortification value is 15: five disciplines, each completed through Rank 3 with their capstones. This requires 300 XP in discipline progression alone.

T-Doll and Human Differences

Humans

Begin with one discipline of their choice, its perk, and one starting technique. Progress through all three ranks normally.

Building Your Own Discipline

Players may design a custom discipline with GM approval. A homebrew discipline should follow these guidelines:

  1. Form a cohesive concept. The discipline should represent a single, focused skillset. If the concept could be expressed as a combination of two existing disciplines, use those instead.
  2. Select three associated skills. These define how the discipline progresses and what kind of character it supports.
  3. Design at least 10 techniques, with a minimum of 3 at each rank. More techniques give the discipline breathing room. At only 10 techniques, technique XP covers 30 of the 60 XP needed to reach the capstone.
  4. Define a perk and a capstone. The perk is granted when the discipline is first added. The capstone is the reward for completing Rank 3.
  5. Get GM approval. Following these guidelines does not guarantee the discipline will be allowed in play. The GM has final say.
