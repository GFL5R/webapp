Driving & Vehicles

What Are Vehicles?

Vehicles allow characters to travel faster than on foot and engage in high-speed chases. In chase scenes, vehicles are treated as extensions of their drivers, with their own damage thresholds and armor values.

Driver

Controls the vehicle, makes all acceleration decisions, and performs driving techniques during chases.

Passengers

May use normal "on foot" techniques, provided they can be performed from within the vehicle.

Vehicle Combat & Chases

Scene Speed

Rather than tracking individual vehicle speeds, each chase has a scene speed that represents the relative intensity and pace of the entire chase. Scene speed is abstract: scene speed 5 on a motorcycle looks wildly different than scene speed 5 in a cargo truck, but both represent the same level of chase intensity.

The scene speed determines the TN for all techniques used during the chase.

Driver Actions

Each round, on their turn, every driver can choose one of the following as a movement action:

ActionEffect
Accelerate Increase the scene speed by 1 (if it hasn't increased this round). Any driver who doesn't also accelerate gains 1 stack of Losing Ground.
Hold Steady Maintain current pace. If any other driver accelerated this round, you gain 1 stack of Losing Ground.
Decelerate Willingly gain 1 stack of Losing Ground (typically to reduce the difficulty of techniques).

Losing Ground

Losing Ground Stacks

  • 3 stacks = Out of the chase. Chasers lose their quarry. Pursued characters are caught.
  • TN Reduction: Your stacks of Losing Ground reduce the TN of techniques you perform during the chase (maneuvers are easier at slower relative speeds).
  • Failed Checks: Failing a technique check during a chase gives you 1 stack of Losing Ground.

Optional Rule: Extended Chases

If all vehicles in the scene have at least 1 stack of Losing Ground, the GM may remove 1 stack from all participants and reduce the scene speed by 1. Use this rule if you want chases to last longer.

Vehicle Durability

Armor Value
Damage dealt to a vehicle is reduced by its armor value (minimum 0).
Damage Threshold
If a vehicle reaches its damage threshold, it is knocked out of the chase.
Targeting the Driver
Drivers can be targeted directly but benefit from their vehicle's armor value in addition to any personal armor. If the driver becomes incapacitated, the vehicle is knocked out of the chase.

Ending a Chase

A chase ends when:

  1. All pursuers or pursued are knocked out (through Losing Ground, vehicle destruction, or driver incapacitation).
  2. A Finishing Technique is successfully used:
    • Chased-type: Can only be used by the pursued to evade the chase.
    • Chaser-type: Can be used by any participant to knock out a vehicle.

Being Knocked Out

Exit TypeConsequence
Losing Ground (3 stacks) Relatively controlled exit. Occupants suffer no immediate harm, though narrative consequences may apply (being caught, losing the target, etc.).
Violent Knockout All occupants take a Critical Strike with severity equal to the current scene speed. The vehicle crashes, spins out, or otherwise comes to a sudden, dangerous stop.
