Electronic Warfare

In 2070, the global network is a patchwork of point-to-point microwave relays, local mesh networks, and hardened fiber backbones. Satellite links are rare and jealously guarded. Most secure systems are air-gapped, connected to wider networks only at scheduled intervals or through physical terminals. To hack something important, you usually have to be close enough to touch it—or close enough to get shot trying.

Electronic warfare follows the same Roll and Keep rules as every other check. Choose an Approach and a relevant Skill, roll dice, keep up to your Approach rating, compare successes to a TN.

Relevant skills most often include Computers for network and system work, Subterfuge for physical bypass and social engineering, Mechanics for hardware interfaces, and Insight for reading network traffic and pattern recognition.

Nodes & Connections

Every hackable network is a map of nodes connected by links. A node represents a single device or cluster of related devices: a camera bank, a locked door, a drone relay, a server, a security panel. Each node is a place in cyberspace where a hacker can act.

Links connect nodes to one another. Some links are open; others are protected by barriers that must be bypassed before a hacker can cross them. A hacker's avatar begins at the node corresponding to the device they physically connected to, and must traverse links to reach other nodes.

Designing a Network

When preparing a network for play, the GM should decide:

  • Nodes: What devices exist on this network? Give each node a brief label (Security Cameras, Server Room, Drone Bay, Main Door).
  • Links: Which nodes connect to which? Draw lines between them.
  • Barriers: Are any links locked? A barrier requires a check to bypass.
  • Security Rating: The network's overall defensive strength.
  • Sentries: Does the network have autonomous defense programs or a human operator?

Security Rating

Every network has a Security Rating from 1 to 6:

RatingExamples
1Consumer home network, unencrypted personal devices
2Small business, street gang hideout, civilian drones
3Corporate branch office, police precinct, military surplus
4Corporate high-security network, government facility
5Military installation, IOP black site, Sangvis Ferri relay
6KCCO command net, Paradeus node, Agency zero-day vault
TN = Security Rating

The GM may adjust by +1 or −1 for specific circumstances (especially hardened node, outdated firmware, insider credentials), but the Security Rating is the baseline. Non-hostile actions on authorized systems are TN 1 or require no check.

Characters who engage in electronic warfare have an EW Rating representing their hardware, software, and exploits:

T-Dolls as Hackers

T-Dolls are electronic devices and potential hackers. A T-Doll has both a Security Rating (for resisting intrusion) and an EW Rating (for performing electronic warfare). They can interface with networks directly through physical connection or short-range wireless link, and do not require a separate cyberdeck.

The Intrusion Scene

An intrusion into a hostile network is a conflict scene using the standard phase structure:

Phase 1: Initiative

Each participant makes a TN 1 Computers check. Base initiative is their Focus (if prepared) or Vigilance (if caught off-guard). On success, add 1 plus bonus successes. The GM also rolls initiative for sentries and operators.

Phase 2: Take Turns

Characters act in initiative order, highest to lowest. Each turn follows:

  1. Beginning of Turn: Resolve beginning-of-turn effects.
  2. Set Stance: Choose your Approach for this turn.
  3. Perform Action: Take one action from the EW Actions list or use a Technique. You may also traverse one link before or after your action (equivalent to moving one range band). Traversing a barrier requires an action instead.
  4. End of Turn: Resolve end-of-turn effects. Then gain Heat.

Phase 3: Resolution

The intrusion ends when the hacker achieves their objective, is forced to disconnect, or chooses to withdraw.

Heat

Heat is a per-character resource that tracks how much noise you're making on a hostile network. It begins at 0 and rises as you act.

Gaining Heat

  • At the end of your turn, if you performed an action against a hostile system, gain 1 Heat.
  • If you failed a check against a hostile system, gain 1 additional Heat (stacks).
  • Some Techniques include a heat cost.

Heat Thresholds

HeatStateEffect
0–3 Unseen The network has not noticed you. No additional effects.
4–6 Noticed The system flags anomalous activity. GM may have sentries begin patrolling or operators log in. No mechanical penalty yet.
7–9 Hunted The network actively interferes: increase TN of your checks by 1 while at this threshold or above.
10+ Burned Forcibly disconnected, physical location traced, on-site security alerted. Your intrusion is over.

Managing Heat

Running Silent
At the start of your turn, declare you are running silent. While running silent, you do not gain Heat at end of turn for performing actions (you still gain Heat from failed checks). However, increase TN of all your checks by 1. You may stop running silent at any time before making a check.
Opportunity
When you generate Opportunity (OP) on a check against a hostile system, spend OP to reduce Heat gained from that check by 1 per OP spent (can reduce to 0).
Pause
The Pause action reduces Heat directly (see EW Actions below).

Electronic Warfare Actions

The following actions are available to all characters in an EW scene—no special training required. More specialized capabilities require Techniques.

Crack
Activation

Scheme action — Computers check at network TN, targeting one system reachable from your current node.

Effect

Gain a foothold on the target system. While you hold this foothold, you may perform actions against this system without needing to Crack again. Lasts until disconnect or forced removal.

Opportunity

OP Leave no entry logs; reduce Heat by 1.
OP OP Discover a backdoor: a hidden link bypassing one barrier to an adjacent node.

Bypass
Activation

Scheme action — Computers check at network TN to unlock a barrier on an adjacent link.

Effect

The barrier is removed for the rest of the scene. You may immediately traverse the link as part of this action.

Opportunity

OP No Heat gained from this check.
OP OP Relock the barrier behind you, sealing it against pursuit.

Disrupt
Activation

Attack action — Computers check at network TN, targeting one device, sentry, or character on your current or adjacent node (where you hold foothold).

Effect

Target suffers Dazed. If the target is a device, it is disabled for 1 + bonus successes rounds.

Opportunity

OP Disruption cascades to one other device on the same node (narrative opening, no mechanical effect).
OP OP Inflict Immobilized instead of/in addition to Dazed. Affected sentries cannot traverse links.

Scan
Activation

Support action — Computers check at network TN.

Effect

GM reveals the layout of all nodes and links within two links, including the presence (but not type) of barriers, sentries, or operators.

Opportunity

OP Identify the specific type and capabilities of one sentry or device within scan range.
OP OP Discover a vulnerability; your next check against that system has TN reduced by 1.

Pause
Activation

Support action — No check required.

Effect

Reduce your Heat by 1 and remove 1 Strife.

Defend
Activation

Support action — Computers check at network TN, targeting yourself, one ally, or one device on your current node.

Effect

Until your next turn, increase TN of hostile EW checks targeting the defended system by 1, plus 1 per two bonus successes. Functions like the Guard action in a skirmish.

Sentries & Operators

Sentries

Autonomous defense programs that patrol or guard nodes. They follow programming with rigid precision.

SentryIntegrityAttackSecurityTypical Behavior
Watchdog432Alert, then attack intruders
Cerberus843Alert, attack, pursue intruders
Manticore1254Alert, attack, lock barriers, pursue
Hydra1865Alert, attack all in node, pursue, reboot devices

Sentries use simplified initiative equal to their Security Rating. When a sentry attacks, it deals damage equal to half its Attack Rating (rounded up) plus bonus successes.

Operators

An operator is a human (or T-Doll) hacker defending the network. Operators use full character rules with Approaches, skills, stances, and techniques. They do not need to Crack systems on their own network, can traverse links freely (ignoring barriers), and do not accumulate Heat on their home network.

T-Dolls & Electronic Warfare

Targeting a T-Doll

A T-Doll can be targeted by EW actions just like any networked device. The attacker must either be on the same network or within range 0–2 with a wireless-capable deck. The TN for hostile actions is the T-Doll's own Security Rating.

Level II

T-Dolls can project their neural cloud directly into a network, entering an immersive virtual layer called Level II. This commits their full processing power, gaining enormous capability at the cost of physical vulnerability.

While in Level II

  • Increase your EW Rating by 2.
  • Reduce your Security Rating by 1 (minimum 0).
  • Your physical body is unresponsive and treated as Immobilized.

Leaving Level II safely requires spending your action on your turn. If your connection is severed unsafely (Burned Heat, forced disconnect, physical line cut), your neural cloud becomes trapped:

While Trapped

  • Your physical body is treated as Incapacitated.
  • You cannot act in physical scenes until your connection is restored.
  • Your neural cloud exists as a vulnerable target on the network.
  • Rescue requires another hacker to reach your node and succeed on a TN 4 Computers check.

Hacker Duels

When two hackers meet with hostile intent, the encounter becomes a Hacker Duel (following standard duel rules with modifications):

Initiative
A TN 1 Computers check (rather than Resolve).
Staredown
At the start of each round, both participants gain Heat equal to the current round number. Then participants who are not Compromised may bid Strife to increase initiative.
Range
Both hackers are at range for all effects. Physical positioning is irrelevant.
Ending
Ends when one participant disconnects for any reason: Burned Heat, voluntary disconnect, Incapacitated, or physical connection severed.

Connecting to a Network

Connection TypeDetails
Physical Hardline cable from cyberdeck (or T-Doll frame port) to a network device. Full capability, no penalties.
Wireless Connect to a device within range 2 with line of sight. TN +1 on all EW checks. Avatar cannot move beyond the connected node.

Connections persist until you voluntarily disconnect, your cable is physically removed, you move out of wireless range, or you are forcibly disconnected.

EW & Physical Scenes

Splitting Actions
A jacked-in character can act in either the physical or virtual realm on their turn, but not both. Movement can be spent in whichever realm they act in.
Awareness
A connected character remains aware of physical surroundings unless in Level II. They can speak, observe, and react, but physical action requires dedicating their turn.
Embedding EW in Other Scenes
An intrusion can run concurrently with a skirmish, chase, or intrigue. Use the same initiative order; each character declares which realm they're acting in.
