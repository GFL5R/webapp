Experience Points

Overview

Each session awards:

  • Party XP for shared progress and overall engagement.
  • Accolade XP for standout individual contributions.

A typical session yields 4 to 6 XP.

Party XP

Awarded equally to all players at the end of the session.

Progress (2 XP)

Granted when the group meaningfully advances its goals for that session. Goals may be dramatic, investigative, social, or recreational. A lighthearted interlude still counts when it reflects what the players set out to do.

Engagement (1–2 XP)

Granted when the table stays attentive and involved. Low energy, quiet roleplay, or slower pacing are acceptable. Reserving XP is appropriate only when players are checked out or intentionally disruptive. Use judgment; distinguish fatigue from disinterest.

Individual Accolades

Each player votes for someone who strengthened the session through roleplay, decision-making, problem-solving, or character development. The player with the most votes gains 1 XP. If multiple players tie, each gains 1 XP.

Total Accolade XP across the table is capped at 2 XP.

Catch-Up Adjustment

At the end of each session, any character more than 10 percent below the party's average XP gains 1 bonus XP. This continues until the gap closes.

Impact

A character creates impact when their actions shift circumstances, reveal information, guide decisions, or contribute meaningfully to the atmosphere or narrative. Spotlight size does not determine impact; relevance does.

Spending XP

Skills

Increasing a skill counts toward the progression of any discipline that lists that skill as an associated skill. Increasing a skill costs twice the new rank in XP.

Techniques

Learning a technique costs 3 XP. A character must meet the technique's rank requirement within the relevant discipline before purchasing it. XP spent on techniques counts toward the progression of the discipline that offers the technique.
