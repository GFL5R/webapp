Factions & Powers

The world of 2072 is shaped by the interplay of governments, corporations, paramilitary groups, and criminal organizations. Understanding these factions is essential for navigating the political and physical landscape your characters inhabit. None of them are entirely good or entirely evil: they are institutions run by people with competing interests, and your characters will inevitably be caught between them.

The Union of Rossartrist Nations Coalition (URNC)

The URNC is the dominant world government, formed in November 2064 in the aftermath of World War Three. Born from the Rossartrist ideology that promised unity and reconstruction, the Coalition absorbed most nations on Earth, with its core territory spanning the Eurasian continent. The Neo-Soviet Union formally merged into the URNC by 2071.

The Coalition is genuinely rebuilding civilization. Its Purification Project, launched in 2067 under the Geneva Protocol, uses Ark-class seeding platforms and mobile purification towers to push back contamination zones from both east and west, aiming to meet in the Carpathians. By 2072, most safety zones in South America, Europe, and northern Asia have been integrated. Refugee programs evacuate Yellow Zone populations to Green Zone satellite cities. Infrastructure expands. The mail gets delivered.

But the URNC is also an authoritarian surveillance state. Its twin AIs, Heli and Iphi, serve as a centralized information-gathering and decision-making system. European conglomerates have established quasi-monopolies in key markets under the Coalition's umbrella. Independent forces are regulated through agencies like NOMFA, and PMCs have been forcibly downsized or disbanded. Former PMC members, including your characters, exist in the cracks of this system.

Key URNC Organizations

  • Central Defense Bureau (CDB): The URNC's supreme military command, controlling all military branches, national security, intelligence, and Relic Technology research.
  • Non-Military Forces Administration (NOMFA): The agency regulating non-state armed groups such as PMCs and bounty hunters. Kryuger and Kalina joined NOMFA after Griffin's disbandment in 2065. NOMFA issued the Mephisto Agreement that prevents former Griffin Dolls from contacting their Commander.
  • Public Security Administration (PSA): Civilian police force in Neo-Soviet Green Zones, making heavy use of Dolls and Automatons. Each city sector has a local Department of Public Security ("PubSec").
  • Special Security Administration (SSA): Handles special police operations.
  • Statesec: State security and intelligence. Maintains a Criminal Investigation and Response Department with its own Public Security Management Section.
  • B.R.I.E.F.: Established in 2067, the top governing body for bounty hunters. Manages contracts, licensing, and oversight of independent operators working in contamination zones.

Within the URNC's Green Zones, the former Neo-Soviet Oblast system remains in use. Each Oblast is led by a Chief and governed alongside an elected Parliament of legislators. This local governance creates room for corruption, factional politics, and corporate influence that player characters can exploit or oppose.

Griffin & Kryuger

Griffin & Kryuger was once the most prominent private military company in Eastern Europe, known for employing Tactical Dolls. Founded in 2053 by former Ministry of Internal Affairs officer Berezovich Kryuger and Rossartrist agent Griffin Lyons, G&K built its reputation fighting the rampaging Sangvis Ferri Dolls after the 2061 Butterfly Incident.

That reputation is now a fading memory. After the catastrophic Great Retreat of 2064, where their KCCO allies betrayed them, the Battle of the Dead Sea, and the Battle of Frankfurt, the URNC forcibly disbanded and reorganized G&K into a small, tightly regulated PMC under Helian's command. Kryuger and Kalina left to join NOMFA. Most T-Dolls were discharged: some took civilian jobs, others went independent, and a few stayed on under Griffin's diminished banner.

By 2072, Griffin is a shadow of its former self. It retains capable land, air, and marine vehicles, and several squads of T-Dolls, but its commissions are few and its finances precarious. Other PMCs have started claiming to be Griffin offshoots, leeching off the old reputation while running shady contracts with cheap Automatons built on now-public-domain Sangvis Ferri technology. The real Griffin soldiers under Helian carry on with quiet professionalism, but the golden age of the PMC is over.

One of Griffin's remaining active squads, Frostfall is led by Qiongjiu (formerly QBZ-191) and includes Tololo (AK-Alfa), Cheeta (MP7), and Lind (AA-12). They handle security contracts in the Green Zones, often struggling with tight budgets and collateral damage from Cheeta and Lind's more enthusiastic approaches to problem-solving.

The Girard Group

The Girard Group is a powerful industrial conglomerate based in the satellite city of Odesa. Led by Rurik Girard, who also holds a seat in the Parliament of Odesa Oblast, the company spans consumer and military markets and is deeply involved in the Purification Project. Girard also operates charitable foundations, cultivating a favorable public reputation. Their relationship with other major conglomerates, particularly Cecht FABN, is the subject of ongoing speculation.

Behind the respectable facade, there are persistent rumors that the Girard Group's rapid advances in Third-Generation Doll technology aren't entirely their own work. Their corporate armed forces include advanced third-gen combat Dolls that some engineers quietly note bear suspicious similarities to 16LAB designs. The company exerts considerable influence over Odesa's government and economy, and with their purification contract over Odesa expired, Statesec suspects they may attempt to maintain control through less legitimate means. NOMFA has been sent to investigate.

The Girard Group's ambitions extend beyond Odesa: they aim to expand their influence to other safe zones across Eastern Europe. For player characters operating in the region, Girard is the kind of faction you can't avoid: they're in the government, they're in the economy, they're in the supply chains, and they have the firepower to back it all up.

Cecht FABN

Cecht FABN is a pharmaceutical conglomerate with murky ties to the Girard Group and other major corporate players. While less publicly visible, Cecht FABN wields enormous influence through its control of medical supplies, particularly ELID vaccines essential for survival in contamination zones. The quality of their cheaper vaccine products is quietly questioned by Yellow Zone medical providers, and their pricing practices ensure that reliable protection against ELID remains a luxury for many.

Cecht FABN's security arm is Mangi Security Service, ostensibly a small PMC registered with NOMFA for under 100 employees. In reality, Mangi employs around 2,000 well-equipped human mercenaries, including an elite force called the "Omen" specializing in Paradeus cleanup operations. The Omen earned their grim name from being constantly called to fight Boojums and other monstrosities, and are equipped accordingly with heavy weapons and IFVs.

In 2071, Mangi collaborated with the Girard Group on the mysterious Project Coronal Hole, recruiting former Griffin T-Dolls as research subjects for third-generation Doll development. This makes them both a potential employer and a very real threat to player characters who are former G&K personnel.

IOP Manufacturing

Important Operations Prototype Manufacturing Company (IOP) is the world's leading Tactical Doll manufacturer, directed by Havier Witkin. Originally formed from a Pan-European Alliance military project merging Scheppel Advanced Technology and Westchester United Electronics, IOP grew to dominate the T-Doll market through its partnership with the research arm 16LAB, led by the brilliant scientist Persica.

After Sangvis Ferri's collapse in 2061, IOP held a virtual monopoly on T-Doll production. By 2072, they supply everyone from national defense forces to private security firms to individual households. IOP produced both Griffin's combat T-Dolls and the Elmo landship, described as "the final glory of the NSU's heavy industry." Their Third-Generation Doll technology, developed through 16LAB, represents the cutting edge of artificial intelligence and combat capability, though the Girard Group has been stealing their base-layer technology for their own inferior copies.

IOP is not a neutral party. Through Witkin's involvement with the Prometheans, IOP provided extensive logistical support during the events of 2064, and Witkin was sympathetic enough to the Commander's cause to stagger the activation of the Nidhogg Protocol, saving many G&K Dolls. In 2072, IOP remains a powerful background player: the company that makes the bodies your T-Dolls wear and the technology that keeps them alive.

Paradeus Remnants

Paradeus was once the most dangerous cult of the 21st century: a German terrorist organization that fused doomsday religious fanaticism with cutting-edge biotechnology. Originating from the Evangel Portal doomsday cult, it was led by the Oberstein family and developed the Nyto cyborg soldiers, blurring the line between human and machine. They pursued Relic Technology through horrific experiments and allied with General Carter's KCCO faction.

Paradeus was largely destroyed during the Battle of the Dead Sea and the Battle of Frankfurt in 2064. Their main base in Germany fell. Their leadership was killed or scattered. A 2071 article described their disappearance as so sudden that scavengers found abandoned strongholds still full of resources.

But Paradeus is not truly dead. By 2072, remnant sects persist in the Red and Yellow Zones of Eastern Europe. They recruit ELID sufferers from contamination zones by promising a cure for their condition, building communities of devoted followers. Their technology has degraded significantly: instead of the formidable Nytos of the 2060s, the remnants appear to field lower-grade cyborg soldiers with combat abilities closer to those of a standard T-Doll, serving as local leaders for Yellow Zone cult communities.

Intelligence on the current Paradeus leadership is thin. Someone is organizing the remnant sects, but who and toward what end remains unclear. Rumors circulate of continued Collapse radiation experiments on followers, and some Yellow Zone operators suspect corporate money is flowing to the sects, though no one has proven the connection. For player characters, Paradeus represents an enemy that should be dead but refuses to stay buried: diminished but still dangerous, still experimenting, still recruiting the desperate.

Sangvis Ferri (Defunct)

Sangvis Ferri was a Romanian arms manufacturer turned rogue AI faction. In 2061, their AI mainframe Elisa turned all SF T-Dolls against humanity in the catastrophic Butterfly Incident. For years, Griffin & Kryuger fought to contain them in Area S09.

By 2067, the Sangvis Ferri rebellion was completely quelled and their product technology patents entered the public domain. As a manufacturer and as a military threat, Sangvis Ferri no longer exists in 2072. However, their legacy is everywhere:

  • Public domain technology: Cheap Automatons built on SF patents flood the market. Shady PMCs and fake Griffin offshoots use them extensively. They lack the Neural Cloud sophistication of true T-Dolls but are much cheaper to produce and deploy.
  • Assimilated Dolls: Some captured SF Ringleaders were modified with restriction protocols and served alongside G&K before the disbandment. Their current status varies.
  • Cultural memory: The Butterfly Incident shaped public attitudes toward T-Dolls and AI autonomy. Fear of another Elisa is part of why the URNC regulates Doll usage so strictly.

The Varjagers

The Varjagers are the Yellow Zone's most persistent headache. Named after the Varangians — medieval Norse mercenaries — they first appeared in 2066 as scattered gangs of deserters and looters picking over contamination zones that nobody else wanted. Six years later, they've grown into a loose confederation of bandit groups spread across Eastern Europe, unified mostly by their Viking-themed affectations: Norse unit names, gas masks, heavy protective suits, and a shared taste for brutality.

The URNC tolerates them because they're smart enough not to raid purified regions. The Varjagers stick to Yellow Zone settlements and convoys, hit the Intercontinental Railway when they think they can get away with it, and prey on anyone unlucky enough to be traveling without adequate firepower. They deal with Underground Brokers when it suits them and rob them when it doesn't. The only communities they leave alone are the contamination sects, whether out of superstition or some pragmatic arrangement that nobody's been able to confirm.

Don't mistake "disorganized" for "harmless." Individual Varjager bands range from desperate scavengers with salvaged rifles to well-armed raiding parties fielding assault mechs — they've had those since 2070, and they know how to use them. Known leaders include Berserl, Ravn, and Lavrentiy, but there's no single warlord holding the whole thing together. That's what makes them unpredictable: each group has its own territory, its own grudges, and its own appetite for violence. For bounty hunters working the Yellow Zone, the Varjagers are the bread and butter — the contracts that keep the lights on between the jobs that might actually kill you.

Varjager Capabilities

Any given Varjager encounter is a coin flip. A small group might be half-starved raiders with improvised weapons; the next could be a disciplined squad with assault mechs, mounted guns, and a plan. They fight dirty as a rule: hostages, ambushes, traps laid in contaminated terrain they know better than anyone. The one constant is that they are always more familiar with the ground than you are.

Underground Brokers

The Underground Brokers are an informal network of traders, fixers, and suppliers operating primarily in the Yellow Zone. They serve as the economic backbone of life outside the Green Zone, brokering deals for supplies, information, transport, and services that the URNC's official economy doesn't reach.

Individual brokers vary enormously in trustworthiness and capability. Some, like Poludnitsa, maintain reputations for fair dealing and merchant solidarity. Others are willing to sell anyone out for profit. The brokers hold annual fairs where they trade goods, compete in delivery rallies, and network. They are the people who know where to find what you need in the wasteland, and they usually want something in return.

For bounty hunters and independent operators, the Underground Broker network is indispensable. They provide supplies, intelligence, and connections that would be impossible to obtain through official channels. Springfield's Zucchero Café operates as both a legitimate business and an intelligence node within this network, demonstrating how the line between honest commerce and espionage blurs in the Yellow Zone.

Bounty Hunters & B.R.I.E.F.

The collapse of the PMC industry after 2065 created a surge of independent operators. Former soldiers, former PMC personnel, and assorted mercenaries turned to bounty hunting in the contamination zones, the last frontier where independent armed work remains viable. By 2067, the Bureau for the Regulation, Investigation, and Enforcement of Freelancers (B.R.I.E.F.) emerged as the top governing body for bounty hunters, managing contracts, licensing, and minimal oversight.

Bounty hunters operate under B.R.I.E.F. contracts, taking jobs that range from escorting convoys through contaminated territory to clearing ELID nests to tracking down wanted criminals. The work is dangerous, the pay is inconsistent, and the legal protections are minimal. Many bounty hunters operate from mobile bases or travel between settlements, living on the margins of URNC control.

This is likely where your characters fit in. Bounty hunting offers freedom from URNC surveillance and corporate politics, at the cost of constant danger, uncertain resources, and the knowledge that the frontier shrinks every year as the Purification Project advances. The question isn't whether the bounty hunter life will end, but what comes after.

Contamination Sects

Throughout the Yellow and Red Zones, various contamination sects have formed around beliefs tied to Collapse radiation, ELID, and survival. Some are harmless communities of the desperate. Others are fronts for Paradeus recruitment, promising cures for ELID in exchange for devotion. The Varjagers notably avoid attacking these settlements, suggesting either superstition or pragmatic agreements.

ELID sufferers in the contamination zones face a grim choice: slow deterioration, expensive vaccines of questionable quality, or the dubious promises of cults that may or may not be connected to an organization that experiments on its own followers. Player characters will encounter these communities in the Yellow Zone, and the moral calculus of how to deal with them is rarely simple.
