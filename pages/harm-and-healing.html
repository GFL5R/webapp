Harm & Healing

Combat and hardship inflict harm in many forms: exhaustion, trauma, and the risk of catastrophic injury. The system tracks harm primarily through Fatigue, with critical injuries and conditions representing more severe consequences.

For complete condition definitions, see Conditions.

Fatigue

Fatigue is the primary measure of harm suffered by both humans and T-Dolls. It represents accumulated stress, pain, and the physical or mental toll of violence and exertion.

  • Fatigue is applied by a weapon's Damage value in combat.
  • Out of combat, strenuous activities (forced marches, hard labor, environmental hazards) also inflict Fatigue.

A character can suffer Fatigue up to their Endurance value. When Fatigue exceeds Endurance, the character becomes Incapacitated.

Defending Against Damage

When an effect deals damage to a character even after reductions, the character must defend against that damage. If they cannot, they suffer a critical strike with severity based on the damage source (the Deadliness, for most weapons).

To defend, the character must receive an amount of Fatigue equal to the remaining damage. If they do not or cannot, they suffer a critical strike with severity based on the source.

Example: Choosing to Take a Critical

A commander is hit by a rifle attack that deals 6 damage after reductions. They have only 3 Endurance remaining before becoming Incapacitated. They may choose not to take 6 Fatigue and instead suffer a critical strike with severity set by the rifle's Deadliness.

Suffering Critical Strikes

A critical strike represents a moment when harm exceeds ordinary fatigue, inflicting lasting trauma or catastrophic damage. Critical strikes have a severity value, determined by the method of injury.

  • Weapons: The weapon's Deadliness sets the severity.
  • Other sources: Environmental hazards, falls, or narrative events may set severity by GM discretion.

Cumulative Critical Strikes

Critical strikes are cumulative during a scene. If a character suffers multiple critical strikes before the scene ends, add their severity values together to determine the total effect on the Critical Hits table below.

Example: Cumulative Criticals

A T-Doll suffers a severity 2 critical strike from a pistol shot, then later in the same firefight takes a severity 3 critical from a rifle. The total severity is 5, resulting in the Severely Wounded condition.

Mitigating Critical Strikes

When a character suffers a critical strike, they may attempt to resist its effects:

  • Make a TN 1 Conditioning check.
  • On success, reduce the severity by 1 plus any bonus successes, to a minimum of 0.
  • This reduction applies to the incoming critical strike before it is added to any existing severity from the scene.

Healing Fatigue

Characters recover from Fatigue through skillful care and rest.

Humans

Healed with the Medicine skill

T-Dolls

Healed with the Mechanics skill

Skill-Based Healing

Make a TN 4 check with the appropriate skill to heal Fatigue. On success, remove 1 Fatigue, plus 1 additional Fatigue per bonus success. Using a Medical Kit or Mechanic Kit reduces this TN to 2.

Example: Field Care with a Kit

You perform emergency treatment using Medicine and a Medical Kit. You roll a TN 2 check and achieve 2 bonus successes: you remove 3 Fatigue total.

End-of-Scene Recovery

At the end of a scene, each character reduces their current Fatigue to half of their Endurance (rounded up) if their Fatigue is above that threshold.

Full Night's Rest

After a full night's rest, a character heals Fatigue equal to two times their Swiftness.

Limits

Critical strikes cannot be healed using the methods above. Criticals require repair, surgery, or other narrative means at the GM's discretion.

Suffocation

T-Dolls cannot suffocate.

If a human character is in an airless environment (such as underwater without a respirator), they begin to suffocate. At the beginning of each of their turns while suffocating, they receive 2 Fatigue and 2 Strife.

If a human is unconscious and suffocating, they must make a TN 3 Conditioning (Resilience) check at the beginning of their turn. If they fail, they perish.
