Humanity & Fame

Alongside Approaches and Skills, every character has two shifting measures that track their inner state and their public image: Humanity and Fame. These are not fixed attributes. They rise and fall based on choices, consequences, and the tone of a character's actions.

Skills describe capability. Approaches describe method. Humanity and Fame describe identity.

Humanity

Humanity measures how deeply a character recognizes themselves and others as people with thoughts, feelings, and intrinsic worth. Characters with high Humanity act with empathy and connection. Characters with low Humanity may become detached, calculating, or ruthless.

Fame

Fame measures how the world perceives the character. High Fame represents pride and admiration. Low Fame represents infamy, suspicion, or fear. While Humanity reflects intention, Fame reflects the values and reactions of observers.

A deeply Human decision might cost all public respect. A calculated or ruthless act may become celebrated. This contrast is a central tension of the game.

Changing Humanity & Fame

Humanity and Fame shift when a character's actions carry emotional weight or public consequence. The following are common triggers; the GM should consider intent, context, and impact.

Increasing Humanity

  • Showing mercy to a defeated opponent who expected none
  • Comforting an ally in crisis, even when it slows the mission
  • Treating a stranger as a person instead of an obstacle
  • Sacrificing time, resources, or safety to protect the vulnerable
  • Undoing harm you caused earlier in the story

Decreasing Humanity

  • Treating people as expendable assets
  • Inflicting suffering for convenience or efficiency
  • Ignoring a plea for help when acting would not endanger the mission
  • Destroying someone's dignity to gain an advantage
  • Rationalizing cruelty as "necessary," especially when alternatives existed

Increasing Fame

  • Completing a mission with notable skill or courage
  • Saving civilians, rescuing allies, or preventing catastrophe
  • Demonstrating exceptional resolve in a public setting
  • Gaining recognition from influential groups or media

Decreasing Fame

  • Failing a public assignment in a way others witness or report
  • Acting dishonorably, disrespectfully, or fraudulently
  • Being blamed for a scandal, tragedy, or betrayal
  • Killing, threatening, or coercing in public view
  • Breaking laws or violating orders in a visible manner

Humanity tracks the inner cost of choices.
Fame tracks the story others tell about you.
