Poisons & Drugs

The dose makes the poison. Almost every substance capable of killing someone is also, in the right quantity, capable of healing them. A character who understands chemistry does not think in terms of medicine versus poison; they think in terms of dose, delivery, and intent.

Doses

Substances are tracked in individual doses. A single dose is the amount needed to produce one application of either a medical or harmful effect. Acquiring doses requires procurement (purchase, theft, extraction) or preparation with a successful Crafting or Science check during downtime. A dose is consumed when applied, whether it works or not.

Administration

Willing Target

No check for basic delivery. A Medicine or Mechanics check may still be required for the medical effect.

Unwilling Target (Covert)

Subterfuge or Deception check to introduce the substance undetected. TN is typically the target's Vigilance, or TN 2 if distracted.

Unwilling Target (Forced)

A physical check to administer by contact, injection, or blade. Treated as a hostile action.

Resistance

When exposed to a harmful substance against their will, make a Conditioning check at the substance's listed TN. On success, the harmful effect does not occur. Bonus successes may reduce duration or severity.

T-Dolls are generally immune to organic poisons but may be vulnerable to chemical processes affecting synthetic materials (solvents, oxidizers, corrosives). The GM determines applicability.

Overdose

Administering multiple doses of the same substance category to a single target within the same scene triggers an overdose:

  • Second dose: The GM may increase resistance TN by 1 and escalate the harmful effect by one degree.
  • Third dose: Automatic overdose regardless of resistance; target suffers a severity 6 critical strike in addition to normal effects.
