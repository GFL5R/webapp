Strife & Emotional Pressure

Strife represents the mounting emotional pressure on your characters: the mix of stress, suppressed feeling, battlefield adrenaline, moral compromise, and cognitive load created by life in 2070 under URNC surveillance. It is universal: humans and T-Dolls accumulate and resolve Strife the same way.

What Strife Does

As Strife rises you edge toward an emotional tipping point. Past your Composure rating you are Compromised: your character is saturated with unprocessed emotion and must either play through that tension or vent it.

◇ EXAMPLE: MOUNTING PRESSURE

A Commander and her T-Doll return from a failed extraction. The player tracks Strife climbing: clipped responses, hard blinking, tightening reload motions for the Doll. None of this needs mechanical effect yet: it is texture signaling a possible break coming soon.

Gaining Strife

Strife can arise from action, environment, or choice.

From Checks

When you keep dice that show the Strife symbol you gain 1 Strife per such symbol. Choosing to keep a die with Strife confirms that the attempt costs emotional energy or neural stability.

From Triggers and Situations

Gain Strife when:

  • You encounter an Anxiety and push through it.
  • You are blocked from pursuing a Passion you just invoked.
  • You witness cruelty, injustice, or needless loss tied to your values.
  • You accept collateral damage, friendly injury, or destructive shortcuts.
  • You override ethical protocols (T-Doll) or personal boundaries (human) for tactical gain.
  • You fail a high stakes check at TN 4+ and stakes remain unresolved.
  • The Guide introduces harsh environmental pressure.
  • You voluntarily lean into drama: take 1–3 Strife for strong emotional portrayal.

From Social and Interpersonal Play

Gain Strife when you suppress a genuine response to maintain tactical calm, when you swallow an argument, or when you perform a role inconsistent with internal belief. The Guide can offer 1 Strife as a prompt when you gloss over emotion. You may decline.

Comparing Strife to Composure

Composure is a derived attribute expressing how much emotional pressure you can absorb before performance degradation. While Strife is less than or equal to Composure you are Stable. When Strife exceeds Composure you become Compromised.

Optional guideline (purely narrative): when Strife reaches half your Composure (rounded up) you are Strained. Portray tension building. No mechanical penalty.

Narrating Strife

You are encouraged to narrate sensory and behavioral tells as Strife accumulates: breathing rhythm shifts, clipped answers, eye tracking variance, motor overcorrection, microtremor, ritual fidget, loss of conversational patience, harsh magazine slap, protective posture near a bonded ally.

Stress Tells

During character creation define at least one stress tell: a subtle outward sign that mounting pressure is bleeding through the facade. Examples: tapping a knuckle against a sidearm, reciting serial numbers under breath, compulsive weapon wipe, forced smile that never reaches the eyes, vocal flattening, brief static pop in a T-Doll's speech synthesis.

Removing Strife

Scene Boundary Relief

At the end of a scene reduce Strife until it equals half your Composure (rounded up) if it was higher.

Restful or Centering Activities

The following can remove additional Strife (often all):

  • Pursue a Passion meaningfully during downtime.
  • Engage in quiet maintenance or calibration (T-Doll) with supportive dialogue.
  • Meditate, pray, journal, or decompress in a safe space.
  • Honest bonding conversation between pair members acknowledging recent strain.
  • Structured ritual (tea, cleaning weapons, music practice) performed mindfully.

Venting While Compromised

While Compromised you can remove all Strife by performing a Vent once per scene. Venting is an intentional dramatic release chosen by the player.

Being Compromised

Mechanical Effects

  • Vigilance counts as 1.
  • When making a check you cannot keep dice that show Strife symbols. If all kept dice show Strife you may keep 0 dice (automatic failure).
  • Opponents attempting to read you treat your Strife as +1 TN resistance: paradoxically you are harder to read because you clamp down.

You cease being Compromised as soon as Strife drops to Composure or lower. Vent always clears all Strife.

Vent Actions

Venting is the GFL analogue to unmasking: brief authentic release of emotion or signal overflow. Choose one form suited to the scene. After resolving its effects, remove all Strife.

Vent Actions carry risk. They can shift Humanity or Fame depending on how visible or transgressive the act is.

Override Protocol

You bypass a hard internal rule (T-Doll) or long held personal boundary (human) to achieve effect. Remove all Strife. Pay: lose 1 Humanity or 1 Fame (player choice) unless the act protects innocents clearly.

Expose Vulnerability

You drop your tactical guard, revealing a weakness or truth. Remove all Strife. The next hostile check targeting you has TN reduced to 1. Gain 1 Humanity but risk losing 1 Fame if witnesses are unsympathetic.

Issue Challenge

You demand immediate resolution: duel, contested command roll, or social confrontation. Remove all Strife. Make an immediate check; on success escalate advantage. On failure lose 1 Fame.

Disrupt Order

You deliver a raw outburst or speech outside expected protocol. Remove all Strife. Roll a Social Skill check; on success shift a resisting NPC attitude one step or grant allies +1 die. On failure lose 1 Fame and mark 1 Humanity down if the outburst was cruel.

Tactical Withdrawal

You disengage abruptly. Remove all Strife. Immediately move out of danger or skip one impending harmful consequence. Lose 1 Fame unless withdrawal preserves team safety credibly.

Aggressive Surge

You channel built emotion into sudden kinetic action or decisive command. Remove all Strife. Gain +1 die on a single immediate combat or tactical check and treat one Strife symbol as an Opportunity. Lose 1 Humanity if collateral harm occurs.

◇ EXAMPLE: VENTING UNDER PRESSURE

After a protracted standoff the Commander is Compromised. The player chooses Disrupt Order: a blunt denunciation of an officer's exploitation plan. All Strife is cleared. A Social check succeeds, allies rally, gaining +1 die to a coordinated extraction attempt. Fame drops by 1.

Alternate: Aggressive Surge

The player could have opted for Aggressive Surge instead: a silent hand signal and immediate breach. Strife cleared, +1 die granted to the door charge, but risk of Humanity loss if civilians were endangered.
