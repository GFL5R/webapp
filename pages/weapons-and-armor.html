Weapons & Armor

Weapons and armor in 2072 blend cutting-edge technology with proven designs, providing tactical advantages in the field while carrying the weight of their consequences. From concealed knives to heavy machine guns, and from lightweight vests to powered exosuits, these tools define a character's capabilities in combat and survival.

Weapon Attributes

Weapons use the following attributes to define their performance, detectability, and impact on a target:

AttributeDescription
CategoryThe broad classification of the weapon. Some techniques or equipment interact with specific categories.
NameThe specific model or designation. Most are based on real-world firearms or recognizable equivalents.
SkillThe Skill required to use the weapon effectively (Blades, Firearms, etc.).
Ideal RangeThe range band at which the weapon performs best. Attacking outside this band increases the TN by +1 per band beyond the weapon's ideal range.
DamageThe weapon's base Fatigue output. Bonus successes increase Damage, making precision fire more punishing.
DeadlinessThe severity of critical effects the weapon inflicts. Higher Deadliness means deeper trauma and more severe critical injuries.
GripHow the weapon is held: 1-Handed or 2-Handed. Some weapons list alternative grips.
ThreatHow many range bands away the weapon's fire can be detected. Higher Threat means louder or more visible weapons.
SignatureHow difficult the weapon is to conceal. Sets the TN for Subterfuge checks to hide the weapon on your person.

Suppressors

A suppressor reduces the audible report and muzzle flash of a firearm, making it harder to detect when fired.

  • Threat −2: The weapon becomes significantly quieter, reducing how far the shot can be noticed. Suppressors do not make a weapon silent; they limit how far its presence carries.
  • Signature +1: The weapon becomes bulkier and harder to conceal. The extra length also makes drawing or stowing slightly more awkward.

Readied & Sheathed Weapons

Readied

Physically prepared to fire, swing, or use immediately. During narrative time, characters may ready weapons freely. During combat, readying requires an action unless otherwise specified.

Sheathed

Stowed, holstered, slung, or otherwise not in hand. Sheathing follows the same timing rules as readying.

A character may ready:

  • One pair of one-handed weapons, or
  • One two-handed weapon

Armor

Armor represents protective gear worn to survive hostile environments and incoming fire. Each suit or plate offers a tradeoff between protection, mobility, and concealability.

Armor Attributes

AttributeDescription
WeightHow heavy, restrictive, or encumbering the armor is. Weight affects two areas:
  1. Fatigue from exertion: When taking Fatigue from non-combat sources (running, climbing, forced marches), Weight increases the Fatigue taken.
  2. Concealment TN: Weight serves as the TN for Subterfuge checks to hide the armor beneath clothing.
ProtectionReduces the Damage a character takes from incoming attacks. Protection is applied before bonus successes increase Damage. Even modest armor can keep a character functional under fire.

Armor Profile Guidelines

  • Higher Protection often comes with higher Weight
  • Low-Weight, high-Protection armor exists but is rare or expensive
  • Low-Protection, low-Weight armor is easy to conceal and comfortable to wear

Characters select armor based on mission needs: discretion, mobility, durability, or specialized protection depending on expected threats.
